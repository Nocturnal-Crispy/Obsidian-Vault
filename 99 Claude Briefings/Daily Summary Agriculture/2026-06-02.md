## Livestock

### 1. Drought-Cattle Crisis Continues — Cash Trade Softening, Forced Liquidation Window Narrowing
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, Drought.gov, Brownfield Ag News, DTN Progressive Farmer)

No material change in drought conditions affecting the US cattle herd:

- 79% of beef cow herd remains in drought-affected areas (26 states, 88% of production)
- 63% of all cattle in moderate or more intense drought
- 51% of alfalfa hay in drought — feed availability continues tightening
- DSCI remains above 200 — historic levels
- Cash cattle trade: $255–258 nationally (stable from last week)
- August live cattle futures closed at $240.60, up $1.55
- August feeder cattle futures closed at $351.55, up $3.12 — partial rebound after recent declines
- Ranchers face forced liquidation decisions if pasture conditions don't improve by July
- USDA disaster assistance: $18B disbursed, deadline extended to August

**Strategic Impact:** The clock is ticking toward July forced-liquidation decisions. If rain doesn't materialize in the next 4–6 weeks, ranchers in the southern Plains and Southwest will begin selling breeding stock, which would create a short-term supply surge followed by structural beef shortage through 2028. The slight futures rebound suggests traders aren't yet pricing in worst-case scenarios. USDA's $18B payout and August deadline extension signal the agency expects worsening through summer.

### 2. HPAI Surveillance — Eastward Spread, No New Escalation Events
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (APHIS, CDC, DVM360)

H5N1 HPAI status update:

- Active across 19+ states in poultry and dairy cattle
- Roughly 10 million birds affected in the past month, with the virus advancing eastward
- California: 43 dairy herd re-quarantines after prior release — reinfection pattern emerging
- CDC maintains "low" public health risk assessment
- USDA investing $1B in comprehensive HPAI strategy to protect poultry industry and lower egg prices
- Pasteurization confirmed effective at inactivating H5N1 in milk
- No new human clusters; worker surveillance continues

**Strategic Impact:** The eastward spread pattern and California re-quarantines suggest the virus is more persistent in dairy herds than initially hoped. The $1B USDA investment signals this is being treated as a long-term challenge, not a contained outbreak. No change in CDC risk assessment keeps this at surveillance level rather than emergency. Monitor for any changes in human transmission patterns.

## Crops

### 3. Winter Wheat Crisis Sustained — Structural Supply Deficit Locked In
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, USDA ERS, Farm Progress, AgWeb, ABC News)

No relief for the winter wheat disaster:

- 2026 wheat acreage: **lowest since 1919**
- Winter wheat production: 1,048M bushels — **lowest since 1965/66**, down 25% YoY
- Hard Red Winter: 515M bushels — **down 36% YoY**
- National abandonment rate: 32%
- 70% of winter wheat in drought; 98% of cotton in drought
- Drought covers 62%+ of Lower 48 — worst spring drought on record
- USDA projects total 2026/27 wheat production at 1.561B bushels (down from 1.985B)
- Pro Farmer: biofuel bets meeting rising food shortage fears as USDA slashes forecast

**Market signals (June 1 close):**
- Chicago SRW wheat: $6.08¾, down 1¾c (5-session decline)
- HRW wheat: $6.69, flat after 5 straight losses
- Partial bounce from oversold conditions but acreage collapse not fully priced

**Strategic Impact:** The wheat supply deficit is now locked in — even perfect weather from here won't recover abandoned acres. US wheat exports will decline materially, supporting higher bread and food prices domestically. The "biofuel vs. food" tension is intensifying as corn acres get pulled toward ethanol while wheat supply shrinks. Watch for June WASDE report (expected mid-June) for updated projections.

### 4. Corn and Soybeans — Planting Complete, Drought Risk Window Opening
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA NASS, RFD-TV, Brownfield Ag News)

Corn and soybean planting is complete and ahead of five-year averages. But the drought risk equation is shifting:

- 27% of corn, 30% of soybean production areas in drought — Midwest relatively spared so far
- **Critical window:** Late June–July. If drought expands into Corn Belt during corn pollination, yield losses could be severe
- USDA projects 2026/27 corn at 16.0B bushels, down 6% YoY on area and yield declines
- Markets not yet pricing severe yield failure

**Market signals (June 1 close):**
- July corn: $4.44, down 2¾c
- July soybeans: $11.80¾, down 6c
- Soybean oil: 79.09, up 137 points (biodiesel demand)
- Soybean meal: $326.50, down $3.30

**Strategic Impact:** The corn pollination window (late June–July) is the make-or-break event for 2026 crop year. If drought expands into IL, IN, IA, OH during this period, rapid futures repricing is likely. Current futures prices suggest markets are cautiously bearish on trade uncertainty but not yet pricing drought-driven yield failure.

## Gardening

### 5. Zone 6a/6b (Hermitage, PA): Potential June Rainfall — But Drought Conditions Persist in 24% of State
**Heat:** Low | **Momentum:** SEASONAL | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Penn State Extension, Drought.gov, Old Farmer's Almanac)

PA drought status and June guidance:

- **Current:** 24% of Pennsylvania under active drought (12.6% moderate, 11.6% severe); additional 13% abnormally dry
- **Outlook:** Forecasts suggest significant rainfall expected in June — potential relief for western PA
- **If rain materializes:** Excellent window for establishing warm-season crops and building soil moisture reserves

**June action items (unchanged from yesterday — act now):**
- Deep water 1–1.5"/week minimum; mulch all beds 2–3" for moisture conservation
- Succession sow beans, lettuce, radish every 2 weeks
- Side-dress tomatoes and corn with nitrogen as they size up
- **Pest watch (active):** Spotted lanternfly nymphs, squash vine borer adults (apply Bt or row cover), Japanese beetles approaching, cucumber beetles active
- **Harvest now:** Strawberries, lettuce, radish, peas, garlic scapes
- **Plan ahead:** Start fall brassica seeds indoors (late June transplant target)

**Strategic Impact:** Potential June rainfall could provide meaningful drought relief for PA gardens — but don't count on it yet. Continue irrigation-first strategy until sustained rain arrives. If forecasts hold, this could be the best planting window in weeks. Watch municipal water restriction announcements if drought persists.
