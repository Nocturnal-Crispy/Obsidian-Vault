## Open / Actionable Now

### NSF SBIR/STTR Phase I — Developing Deep Technologies (NSF 26-510 / 26-511)
**Heat: Medium | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: NEW | Type: Grant (non-repayable)**

NSF's FY2026 SBIR/STTR Phase I solicitations are open. Phase I awards provide **up to $305,000** for 6–18 months. The next **full-proposal deadline is July 27, 2026 (34 days away)**, with later windows on Nov 4, 2026 and Mar 4, 2027. **Important process catch:** NSF requires an *invited* Project Pitch before a full proposal can be submitted; new Project Pitches against the FY2026 solicitations could be submitted beginning June 2, 2026. To realistically hit the July 27 deadline, the Project Pitch should go in immediately.

**Eligibility fit:** Strong for the 1099 developer/independent software consultant profile if incorporated as a small business (must be a US for-profit small business, majority US-owned). Deep-tech / R&D framing required — not for routine software consulting work.

Apply: [NSF SBIR Solicitations](https://seedfund.nsf.gov/solicitations/) · [NSF 26-510 full solicitation](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/small-business-innovation-research-small-business-technology/nsf26-510/solicitation)

### USDA SBIR Phase I (NIFA) FY2026 — *unconfirmed, verify*

The **FY2026 Phase I deadline is not yet officially posted**; USDA solicitations are typically expected in the **May–June window**, so a NOFO may be imminent. **Verify on Grants.gov before relying on any date.**

**Eligibility fit (cross-link — both profiles):** This is the bridge between Mike's two profiles — a software/precision-ag tool aimed at farming or food systems could qualify. Set a Grants.gov alert for the NIFA SBIR/STTR NOFO.

Apply / watch: [NIFA SBIR/STTR](https://www.nifa.usda.gov/grants/programs/sbir-sttr) · [SBIR.gov topics](https://www.sbir.gov/topics)

### USDA REAP (Rural Energy for America Program) — Grant component PAUSED
**Heat: Medium | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: RISING (status change) | Type: Grant (paused) / Loan (open)**

USDA announced on **March 31, 2026** that REAP will make **no further grant awards until new regulations are in effect**; the agency is **not accepting REAP grant applications** at this time. REAP **guaranteed loans remain available**. Prior FY2026 grant deadlines (Sep 30 / Dec 31, 2025; Mar 31, 2026) are moot for new grant applicants.

**Eligibility fit:** REAP grants fit on-farm renewable energy / efficiency projects (solar, grain dryers, efficient equipment) — relevant to the farm profile when reopened. Watch for the new rule.
