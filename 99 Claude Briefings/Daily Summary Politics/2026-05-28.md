## 1. Tariff Legal Battle: Administration Rebuilds Wall via Section 301/232
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (Court rulings, USTR)

After the Supreme Court ruled IEEPA cannot authorize tariffs (Feb 20) and the Court of International Trade struck down Section 122 tariffs (May 7, under appeal), the administration is reconstructing tariff authority through expanded Section 301 and 232 investigations:

- **Section 301 investigation** into structural manufacturing overcapacity covering 16 countries and 75%+ of US imports
- **Section 301 investigation** into forced-labor enforcement across 60 economies covering nearly all US imports
- Section 122 authority expires July 24, 2026 unless Congress extends it

Tariffs continue to be collected during appeals.

**Strategic Impact:** The tariff regime is legally fragile but operationally intact. The July 24 expiration is a critical deadline. Agricultural commodity markets are directly affected — corn pulled back on doubts over China's commitment to $17B annual ag purchases.

## 2. Federal Judge Declines to Block Mail-In Voting Executive Order
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 2 (Washington reporting)

A federal judge in Washington, D.C. declined to temporarily block President Trump's executive order calling for restrictions on mail-in voting. This decision allows the order to stand pending further legal proceedings.

**Strategic Impact:** With 2026 midterms approaching, mail-in voting access is a high-stakes electoral issue. Expect continued litigation and state-level responses.

## 4. Trump Approval Erosion Among Non-College White Voters
