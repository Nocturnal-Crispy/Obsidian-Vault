# Gaming Intelligence — Wednesday, April 29, 2026

## 1. Diablo IV: Lord of Hatred Launches — 30K Steam Peak, Two New Classes
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Blizzard, Steam Charts, OpenCritic, PC Gamer)**

Diablo IV's second expansion, Lord of Hatred, launched on April 28 across all platforms with the Season of Reckoning. The expansion adds the Skovos region, two new classes (Paladin and Warlock), War Plans, Echoing Hatred, the Horadric Cube, Talismans, and Fishing. On Steam, Diablo IV hit a year-high peak of 29,998 concurrent players on launch night — a 6% increase over the Season of Slaughter record of 28,420. The number is expected to climb further over the weekend. The Paladin brings holy melee combat while the Warlock introduces demonic chains, flame, and hellish magic. Priced at $39.99 base with Deluxe and Ultimate editions available. Early community reception is positive, with praise for Skovos and the Paladin class in particular.

## 2. Neverness to Everness (NTE) Global Launch — Cross-Platform Urban Open World
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Perfect World Games, PC Gamer, GamingOnPhone)**

Neverness to Everness, the urban open-world RPG from Hotta Studio (Tower of Fantasy developers) published by Perfect World Games, launched globally on April 29 across PC, PS5, Mac, Android, and iOS with full cross-platform support. The game blends anime-style visuals with modern city exploration and supernatural combat. NTE had significant pre-registration numbers and is positioning itself as a competitor in the gacha open-world space alongside Genshin Impact and Wuthering Waves. Launch day player counts are not yet available but the game trended on social media across multiple regions. This is Hotta Studio's second major global launch after Tower of Fantasy.

## 3. Sega Universe Initiative — "No Old, Stay Gold" Revival of 9 Classic IPs
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Sega, Gematsu, Push Square, Nintendo Life, Insider Gaming)**

Sega announced "Sega Universe," a transmedia initiative to revive nine classic franchises under the slogan "No Old, Stay Gold." The first phase centers on 2026 anniversary projects: Fantasy Zone and OutRun (40th), Streets of Rage, Rent A Hero (35th), Guardian Heroes, NiGHTS into Dreams, Dynamite Deka, Sakura Wars (30th), and Segagaga (25th). The initiative extends beyond games into film, music, fashion, and entertainment — spearheaded by Justin Scarpone, former Disney executive hired in 2024 as Sega's global head of transmedia. The strategy mirrors the Sonic the Hedgehog brand expansion model and aims to replicate it across Persona and Like a Dragon pillars. This is Sega's most ambitious legacy IP revival in company history.

## 4. Windrose Dominates Steam — 222K Peak as April's Breakout Pirate Game
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 2 (SteamDB, InGameNews)**

Windrose, developed by Kraken Express, has been April's breakout title on Steam after launching into Early Access. The pirate-themed game reached a peak concurrent count of 222,134 on April 19, maintaining a 24-hour peak of ~153,020 in late April. It has dominated the Steam charts and player attention throughout the month, positioning itself as one of 2026's biggest surprise hits alongside earlier breakouts like R.E.P.O. and Schedule I.

## 5. ARC Raiders: Riven Tides Update — New Map, ARC, and Content
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Embark Studios email)**

Embark Studios released the Riven Tides update for ARC Raiders, adding a new map, new ARC character, and additional playable content. ARC Raiders has maintained a steady player base since its Flashpoint update earlier in the year, which saw a 482K all-time high. The Riven Tides update continues the live-service cadence and aims to sustain momentum through spring.
