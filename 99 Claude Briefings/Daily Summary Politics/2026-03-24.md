## Story 2: DHS Shutdown — Republican Breakthrough Plan, Optimism for Deal This Week
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

After five weeks and five failed Senate votes, a genuine path to ending the DHS shutdown emerged Monday night. Sens. Katie Britt (R-AL), Bernie Moreno (R-OH), Lindsey Graham (R-SC), and Steve Daines (R-MT) proposed a two-step approach:

**Step 1:** Fund all DHS agencies (TSA, FEMA, Coast Guard) except ICE deportation operations — which are already funded through last year's One Big Beautiful Act.

**Step 2:** Address ICE funding and SAVE America Act elements through budget reconciliation (which requires only 51 votes, bypassing the 60-vote filibuster threshold).

Sen. Britt told reporters Monday night: "We do" have a solution. Senators expressed optimism about finalizing legislative text this week.

The pressure is real: TSA officers are quitting or calling off, airport lines are growing, and officials warned some airports may need to close. Senate Majority Leader Thune threatened to cancel Easter recess.

**Why it matters:** This is the most credible off-ramp since the shutdown began. The two-step approach threads the needle — Democrats get to claim they didn't fund contested ICE operations directly, Republicans get full DHS funding and a reconciliation path for their priorities. If Trump agrees, this could resolve within days.

## Story 3: $1B TotalEnergies Offshore Wind Buyout
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

The Interior Department announced the Trump administration will pay $1 billion to TotalEnergies, the French energy company, to walk away from two US offshore wind leases. This is a direct government buyout of renewable energy commitments — paying a foreign company not to build clean energy infrastructure.

**Why it matters:** This sets an extraordinary precedent — using taxpayer money to actively dismantle renewable energy projects rather than merely failing to support them. The $1B expenditure during a DHS shutdown and unfunded $200B war adds to the fiscal incoherence narrative.
