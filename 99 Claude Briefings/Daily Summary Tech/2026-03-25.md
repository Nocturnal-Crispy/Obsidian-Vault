# Tech Intelligence — Wednesday, March 25, 2026

## Story 1: Arm Launches First In-House Silicon — 136-Core AGI CPU with Meta as Lead Partner
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)
**Categories:** GPU / Semiconductor, AI

Arm has crossed the Rubicon. After 35 years as a pure IP licensing company, Arm announced the AGI CPU — a 136-core data center processor it designed, fabbed (TSMC 3nm), and will sell as finished silicon. Meta is the lead partner and co-development collaborator, with plans to deploy the chip alongside its custom MTIA accelerators.

Key specs: Up to 136 Neoverse V3 cores at 3.2 GHz all-core / 3.7 GHz boost, dual-die design, 300W TDP, 12-channel DDR5 at 8800 MT/s delivering 800+ GB/s memory bandwidth. Arm claims 2x performance-per-rack versus latest x86 platforms.

The ecosystem is already broad: Cerebras, Cloudflare, F5, OpenAI, Positron, Rebellions, SAP, and SK Telecom have confirmed commercial commitments. Arm and Meta have committed to a multi-generation roadmap.

**Why it matters:** This is a structural shift in the data center processor market. Arm moving from IP licensor to silicon vendor puts it in direct competition with Intel and AMD for server CPU sockets. The "agentic AI infrastructure" positioning — CPU-side orchestration for AI accelerator farms — is a bet that AI workloads need purpose-built CPU companions, not general-purpose x86. If the performance claims hold, this accelerates the x86-to-Arm migration already underway at hyperscalers.

## Story 2: LiteLLM Supply Chain Attack — TeamPCP Backdoors 97M-Download Package via Trivy Compromise
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)
**Categories:** Security, AI

The most significant AI infrastructure supply chain attack to date. On March 24, the threat actor TeamPCP published backdoored versions (v1.82.7–1.82.8) of LiteLLM, the most popular open-source LLM proxy in the Python ecosystem with ~97 million monthly downloads and presence in 36% of all cloud environments.

The attack chain: TeamPCP first compromised Aqua Security's Trivy GitHub Action on March 19 (CVE-2026-33634, covered in yesterday's briefing). Five days later, when LiteLLM's CI/CD pipeline ran Trivy as part of its build process, the compromised action exfiltrated LiteLLM's PyPI publish token. The attackers then published malicious versions containing a .pth file that executes automatically on every Python process startup.

The malicious versions were live for approximately three hours before PyPI quarantined them. The credential stealer matches TeamPCP's toolkit from the Trivy and GlassWorm (Open VSX) campaigns.

**Why it matters:** This is a cascading supply chain attack — Trivy compromise → LiteLLM compromise — targeting the AI development stack specifically. LiteLLM sits in the critical path of AI inference infrastructure. The three-hour window and 97M monthly downloads means the blast radius could be enormous. Organizations running LiteLLM should audit immediately. This also escalates last week's Trivy story from Medium to Critical.

## Story 3: OpenAI Shuts Down Sora App, Disney Kills $1B Investment Deal
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)
**Categories:** AI, Market Signals

OpenAI is discontinuing its standalone Sora video generation app just six months after launch, and the Disney partnership built around it has collapsed. Disney had agreed to license iconic characters (Mickey Mouse, Cinderella) to OpenAI for Sora and take a $1 billion stake in the company — but no money ever changed hands. ChatGPT will also lose video generation capabilities.

**Why it matters:** This is OpenAI's first major product retreat. Sora was positioned as the future of AI video and the Disney deal was the marquee entertainment partnership. The shutdown signals that either compute costs were unsustainable, user engagement was disappointing, or both. More broadly, this challenges the narrative that generative AI video is ready for commercial prime time. The $1B evaporated deal is a material hit to OpenAI's fundraising narrative at a time when the company is reportedly seeking its next round.

The matter is now under submission — Anthropic filed its declaration by 6 PM Monday, the government's response is due today (March 25) by 6 PM PDT, and a ruling is expected in coming days.

The case remains the most consequential AI governance proceeding of 2026.

## Story 6: Arcade Launches ToolBench — MCP Server Quality Benchmark
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)
**Categories:** AI

Arcade released ToolBench, a benchmark that rates MCP (Model Context Protocol) server quality across enterprise applications. Key finding: Slack, Workday, Meta's ad platform, and WhatsApp rank as the most closed-off to AI agents, while GitHub and Figma are the most open. This is the first systematic measurement of enterprise API openness to agentic AI.

**Why it matters:** As MCP adoption accelerates, the openness gap between platforms will determine which enterprise tools AI agents can actually use effectively. ToolBench provides the first empirical basis for these comparisons.
