## 1. Iran-US Ceasefire: Tentative 60-Day Extension Awaits Trump Approval
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (RFE/RL, CNN, Washington Post)

US and Iranian negotiators have reached a tentative agreement to extend the current ceasefire for 60 days and reopen the Strait of Hormuz to commercial traffic. VP Vance confirmed the US is "very close" to a memorandum of understanding. Iran would have 30 days to remove all sea mines from the strait.

However, Trump has not given final approval. Key demands remain unresolved: Iran must surrender its highly enriched uranium and commit to not pursuing nuclear weapons. Iran's Tasnim News Agency reports the framework text has not been finalized. Tensions persist — fresh strikes were exchanged in the Persian Gulf even during negotiations, and the US Treasury threatened Oman with sanctions over potential fees for Hormuz transit.

The original two-week ceasefire was brokered by Pakistan on April 8, 2026. This extension would represent a significant diplomatic escalation toward a broader deal.

**Strategic Impact:** A Hormuz reopening would immediately ease global oil and shipping costs. Failure would mean resumed hostilities and potential oil price spikes. Agricultural commodity markets (especially fertilizer and fuel costs) are directly exposed. This is the single highest-impact geopolitical development today.

## 2. Tariff Legal Architecture: Section 301 Rebuild Continues
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USTR, Court filings)

No new developments today, but the tariff legal situation remains the dominant domestic trade story:
- Section 122 tariffs expire July 24 unless Congress acts
- Section 301 investigations covering 75%+ of imports continue
- Tariffs continue to be collected during appeals
- The administration is rebuilding tariff authority through 301/232 after IEEPA and 122 legal setbacks

**Strategic Impact:** The July 24 deadline is now 56 days away. Agricultural exports to China remain uncertain — the $17B annual purchase target was downgraded to a "guiding target" by China's Commerce Ministry.
