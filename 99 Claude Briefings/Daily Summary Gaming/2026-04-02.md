# Gaming Intelligence — Thursday, April 2, 2026

## 1. Temtem: Swarm 1.0 Launch Day — Full Release After Extended EA
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Crema Games, Steam)**

Temtem: Swarm hits its full 1.0 release today on Steam after an extended early access period dating back to 2024. The Vampire Survivors-style bullet hell roguelite features co-op gameplay built around the Temtem creature franchise. The 1.0 launch brings the complete content suite. Current concurrent numbers are modest (~47 at time of check), suggesting this remains a niche title — but the full release could generate a second wind if word-of-mouth and streamer attention pick up. The game occupies a unique intersection of monster-taming and survival-roguelite genres.

## 2. Darwin's Paradox — Launch Day
**Heat: Low | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Steam)**

Darwin's Paradox, an action-adventure puzzle platformer, launches today on Steam. Players control an octopus named Darwin escaping a mysterious industrial complex to return to the ocean. This is a smaller indie release — no major pre-launch buzz — but the unique protagonist and platformer mechanics could attract puzzle-platformer enthusiasts. Worth monitoring for any viral discovery moments.

## 3. ARC Raiders Flashpoint — Retention Holding Strong
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Embark Studios), Tier 3 (SteamDB)**

ARC Raiders continues to hold exceptional retention post-Flashpoint update. The game ranks fifth in daily Steam players and is notably the first paid game on the most-played list. With an all-time peak of 482K concurrent, daily peaks remain in the 250K–300K range. Embark's content cadence — new ARCs, weapons, and gameplay loops — is proving that the cooperative extraction shooter formula can sustain engagement beyond launch hype. The game's paid model succeeding in the top-5 concurrent on Steam alongside free-to-play giants is a significant market signal.

## 4. April Release Calendar — Heavy Back Half
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Multiple publishers)**

April's release calendar is skewed toward the second half of the month with major titles stacking up:

- **Hades II** (April 14) — PS5/Xbox/Cloud, day one on Game Pass. Console debut of Supergiant's roguelike sequel. This is the month's flagship release.
- **REPLACED** (April 14) — Cyberpunk 2D action-platformer on Steam. High anticipation from extended development.
- **Mouse: P.I. for Hire** (April 16) — Rubber hose FPS, strong style factor.
- **Pragmata** (April 17) — Capcom's new sci-fi IP on all platforms including Switch 2. The first major third-party test of Switch 2 performance.

The Hades II console launch is the single biggest event this month — expect massive Game Pass engagement and potential for one of the year's highest-rated releases.

## 5. Sony Dynamic Pricing — No Reversal in Sight
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: DECLINING | Source: Tier 2 (Community, PC Gamer)**

Sony's dynamic pricing system continues through the Spring Sale with no signs of reversal. Community backlash has cooled from its initial peak — the conversation is shifting from outrage to resigned acceptance. Neither Microsoft nor Steam have adopted similar systems. The story is declining in momentum as it transitions from a controversy to an entrenched platform policy. Long-term consumer trust impact remains the key metric to watch, especially as Game Pass continues to compete aggressively on value.
