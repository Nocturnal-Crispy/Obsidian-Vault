## Livestock

### 1. Drought-Cattle Crisis — Forced Liquidation Window Narrows Further
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, Drought.gov, Pro Farmer, AgWeb, ABC News)

The drought crisis gripping cattle country continues with no material relief:

- 79% of beef cow herd in drought-affected areas (26 states, 88% of production) — unchanged
- 63% of all cattle in moderate or more intense drought
- "I've never seen anything like this" — producers across the Western Plains describing conditions as generational
- Water supplies dwindling, pastures failing, hay production shrinking
- Cash cattle trade: ~$255–258 nationally (stable)
- Feeder cattle futures: partial rebound continues but structural tightness persists
- USDA disaster assistance: $18B disbursed, deadline extended to August
- **Critical timeline:** Ranchers face forced herd liquidation decisions if conditions don't improve by July — approximately 4 weeks away

**Strategic Impact:** No change in trajectory. The Pro Farmer and AgWeb reporting on Western Plains devastation adds human-scale context to the data: producers are describing this as the worst drought in living memory. The July forced-liquidation window is now less than 4 weeks away. Every day without significant rainfall shortens the decision horizon. If mass liquidation begins, expect a short-term beef price dip (supply surge) followed by structural shortage through 2028 as the breeding herd shrinks.

### 2. HPAI Surveillance — Vaccine Development Progressing, Eastward Spread Pattern Holds
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (APHIS, CDC, USDA)

H5N1 HPAI update:

- Active across 19+ states in poultry and dairy cattle — no change in scope
- USDA's National Milk Testing Strategy: 100 herds across 18 states enrolled in Dairy Herd Status Program
- **New: Vaccine candidates in field trials** — USDA supporting rapid development of H5N1 vaccine for dairy cows and other species. Multiple candidates under evaluation
- California re-quarantines (43 dairy herds) — reinfection pattern continues
- Pasteurization confirmed effective at H5N1 inactivation in milk
- CDC maintains "low" public health risk
- $1B USDA investment in comprehensive HPAI strategy
- Detections higher in fall and spring migration cycles

**Strategic Impact:** The vaccine field trials are the most significant new development — if effective, a dairy cattle vaccine would fundamentally change the HPAI management equation from "contain and cull" to "vaccinate and manage." Timeline to approval is unclear but this shifts the long-term outlook from pessimistic to cautiously hopeful. The eastward spread pattern and California reinfections confirm the virus is persistent in dairy herds, reinforcing that a vaccine is the most viable path to control.

## Crops

### 3. Winter Wheat — Harvest Underway in Southern Plains, Damage Already Visible
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, RFD-TV, Pro Farmer, AgWeb)

Wheat harvest has begun in the Southern Plains and South, confirming earlier damage projections:

- 2026 wheat acreage: **lowest since 1919**
- Winter wheat production: 1,048M bushels — **lowest since 1965/66**, down 25% YoY
- Hard Red Winter: 515M bushels — **down 36% YoY**
- National abandonment rate: 32%
- Early harvest yields in Kansas confirming drought and freeze damage — yields below already-low expectations in some areas
- Total 2026/27 wheat: 1.561B bushels (USDA), below average estimate of 1.747B — **smallest crop since 1972**
- Biofuel vs. food tension intensifying as corn acres get pulled toward ethanol while wheat supply shrinks

**Strategic Impact:** With harvest now actively confirming the damage, the market has moved from "forecasting" to "measuring" the supply deficit. Early Kansas yields below expectations could push the next WASDE report (mid-June) even lower. The wheat supply deficit is locked in — no weather improvement can recover abandoned acres. Watch for export restriction discussions if prices spike further.

### 4. Corn and Soybeans — Planting Complete, Critical Pollination Window Approaching
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA NASS, RFD-TV, Brownfield Ag News)

Corn and soybean status update:

- Planting complete, ahead of five-year averages
- 27% of corn, 30% of soybean production areas in drought — Midwest relatively spared so far
- **Critical window: Late June–July** — corn pollination determines the crop. If drought expands into IL, IN, IA, OH during this period, rapid futures repricing likely
- USDA projects 2026/27 corn at 16.0B bushels, down 6% YoY
- Markets cautiously bearish on trade uncertainty but not pricing severe yield failure
- **New: Forced-labor tariffs (USTR)** could affect trade flows with 60 economies, adding uncertainty to export demand

**Strategic Impact:** The next 3-4 weeks determine the corn crop — and by extension, the entire feed/food/fuel complex. Current drought positions (Midwest mostly spared) give cautious optimism, but the expanding drought footprint across the Lower 48 (62%+) creates risk of westward-to-eastward spread into the Corn Belt. The USTR forced-labor tariff proposal on 60 economies adds a new layer of trade uncertainty to grain export demand.

## Gardening

### 5. Zone 6a/6b (Hermitage, PA): First Week of June — Peak Warm-Season Establishment
**Heat:** Low | **Momentum:** SEASONAL | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Penn State Extension, Drought.gov)

PA drought and seasonal guidance:

- **Current:** 24% of Pennsylvania under active drought (12.6% moderate, 11.6% severe); additional 13% abnormally dry — unchanged
- **June rainfall outlook:** Forecasts continue to suggest potential relief later in June — monitor daily
- **This week:** Peak transplanting window for warm-season crops still open

**June first-week priorities:**
- Continue deep watering 1–1.5"/week; mulch all beds 2–3"
- Succession sow beans, lettuce, radish every 2 weeks
- Side-dress tomatoes and corn with nitrogen as they size up
- **Pest watch (active):** Spotted lanternfly nymphs (check tree of heaven, grape), squash vine borer adults NOW (apply Bt or row cover before they bore), Japanese beetles approaching, cucumber beetles active on squash/cukes
- **Harvest now:** Strawberries (peak), lettuce, radish, peas, garlic scapes
- **Plan ahead:** Start fall brassica seeds indoors by late June

**Strategic Impact:** No material change from yesterday. The potential June rainfall remains the key variable — if it materializes, it provides a critical moisture window for warm-season crop establishment. Until sustained rain arrives, maintain irrigation-first strategy. The squash vine borer window is NOW — preventive action this week prevents losses in July.
