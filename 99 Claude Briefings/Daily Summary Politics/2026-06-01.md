## 1. Iran Ceasefire Extension: Day 4 Without Signature — Lebanon Coverage Emerging as Key Sticking Point
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Axios, The Hill, CBS, PBS, CNN)

The tentative US-Iran 60-day ceasefire extension remains unsigned entering its fourth day of limbo. Key developments:

- Trump "recently edited" the agreement text (CBS sources), confirming active engagement but no finalization
- **New sticking point identified:** Iran insists Lebanon/Hezbollah operations must be covered by any ceasefire — the US has resisted including Israeli operations in the agreement
- Iranian state media confirms the deal is not finalized on their end either
- The deal would reopen the Strait of Hormuz (Iran must clear sea mines within 30 days), lift the US blockade on Iranian ports, and issue sanctions waivers for oil sales
- Iran must surrender highly enriched uranium and commit to no nuclear weapons
- Trump characterized the ceasefire as on "life support" — intermittent fighting continues since May 4
- Treasury Secretary Bessent: sanctions relief would "go very slowly"

The emerging deal has drawn criticism from Trump's own supporters — concerns that Iran's leadership will emerge "battered but emboldened."

**Strategic Impact:** The Lebanon dimension makes this significantly more complex than a bilateral US-Iran deal. Israel's operations against Hezbollah are a separate theater that Iran is attempting to bundle. Every day without signature increases escalation risk. Hormuz closure continues to elevate global shipping costs, energy prices, and fertilizer supply chains. A collapse would spike oil and agricultural input costs immediately.

## 2. Section 122 Tariffs: Court Struck Down, 53 Days to Expiration — Administration Pivoting to Section 301
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1 (CIT ruling, Skadden, Holland & Knight, PwC, Clark Hill)

Major legal development: On May 7, a divided three-judge panel at the U.S. Court of International Trade invalidated the administration's 10% Section 122 tariffs (Proclamation 11012), ruling the President exceeded statutory authority. Key status:

- **Tariffs expire July 24, 2026** (53 days) — the 150-day statutory limit from their February 24 effective date
- Congress can extend beyond 150 days but the President cannot unilaterally — extension considered unlikely
- The administration is **pivoting to Section 301** as its primary long-term tariff mechanism, with multiple country-specific investigations already underway
- Section 301 tariffs expected to be timed to take effect before Section 122 expires
- China's $17B annual agricultural purchase target downgraded to a "guiding target" — effectively uncommitted

**Strategic Impact:** The court ruling + expiration creates a legal and policy transition period. The pivot to Section 301 means tariff authority shifts from temporary emergency powers to investigation-based mechanisms — slower to implement but harder to challenge legally. Agricultural exporters face continued uncertainty as China walks back purchase commitments. Watch for Section 301 investigation timelines and whether they can fill the gap before July 24.

## 3. Trump Base Erosion Among Non-College White Voters — Week 3
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 2 (Polling aggregates, multiple outlets)

White voters without college degrees remain net-negative on Trump's job approval entering a third week. This demographic was the foundation of 2024 victory margins in MI, WI, PA. The erosion correlates with:

- Tariff-driven consumer price increases
- Agricultural product uncertainty
- Cost-of-living pressures from Hormuz-related energy price elevation

**Strategic Impact:** Three weeks of sustained erosion (vs. two weeks reported yesterday) makes this increasingly durable. If this holds through summer, it undermines GOP positioning in competitive House districts for 2026 midterms. Candidates may begin distancing from tariff policy. Watch for tariff messaging shifts in coming weeks.

## 4. USDA Disaster Assistance Extended — $18B Paid, Deadline Pushed to August
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, Farmers.gov)

USDA has paid out nearly $18 billion in supplemental disaster assistance under the American Relief Act (passed by Congress in 2025). The application deadline has been extended to August 2026. Programs include ELAP (Emergency Assistance for Livestock, Honeybees, and Farm-Raised Fish) covering drought-related water and feed transportation costs.

**Strategic Impact:** The extension acknowledges the severity and duration of the drought crisis. $18B already disbursed signals this is approaching historic disaster levels. The August deadline extension suggests USDA expects conditions to worsen through summer. Cross-reference with agriculture briefing drought coverage.
