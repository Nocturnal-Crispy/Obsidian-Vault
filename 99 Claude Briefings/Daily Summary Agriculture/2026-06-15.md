# Agriculture Intelligence — 2026-06-15

## Livestock

### 1. Cattle Prices Continue Surge on Tight Supply
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Confirmed)**

As of June 9: August live cattle at $239.70 (+$2.97), feeder cattle at $354.10 (+$3.45). USDA WASDE estimates 2026 beef production at 25.735B lbs — less than 1% below 2025. Smaller herd sizes are the primary driver. USDA's January inventory confirmed US cattle herd down slightly year-over-year.

**Why it matters:** Tight supply means sustained high prices through 2026. Producers benefit from prices but face elevated feed costs. Consumers face continued beef price inflation at retail.

### 2. HPAI Avian Flu Remains Active in Poultry and Dairy
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Confirmed)**

H5N1 HPAI continues affecting US domestic birds and dairy cattle. As of late 2025, USDA estimated ~184 million egg-laying chickens infected across all 50 states and Puerto Rico. CDC maintains the public health risk is low but monitors dairy and poultry worker exposure. APHIS continues surveillance across commercial flocks, backyard birds, wild birds, and dairy cattle.

**Why it matters:** The scope of poultry losses continues to pressure egg prices and layer flock sizes. Dairy cattle involvement adds a novel dimension to biosecurity planning. No sign of resolution on the horizon.

## Crops

### 3. Soybean Futures Hit 4-Month Low on Favorable Conditions
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Confirmed)**

July soybeans at $11.14/bu (June 12), down to a 4-month low. Favorable US growing conditions and strong planting progress reinforce expectations of ample supply. July corn at $4.19½ (+¾¢). July Chicago wheat at $5.85¼ (+2¢). USDA reports planting 87% complete as of late May — ahead of the 5-year average.

**Why it matters:** Lower soybean prices relieve some feed cost pressure for livestock producers. Strong planting progress reduces weather risk premium in grain markets.

### 4. USDA June Agricultural Survey Underway — Key Reports June 30
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Confirmed)**

NASS contacted 90,000+ producers for the June Agricultural Survey to determine crop acreage and stock levels as of June 1. Data feeds into the Acreage report, Grain Stocks report (both June 30), monthly Crop Production reports, and livestock reports including Cattle, Sheep and Goats, and Hogs and Pigs.

**Why it matters:** June 30 reports are major market-moving events. The Acreage report in particular will set expectations for 2026 crop production and influence grain futures through harvest.

## Gardening (Zone 6a/6b — Hermitage, PA)

### 5. Mid-June Tasks: Peak Planting and Pest Watch
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 (Reputable)**

**What to do now:**
- Final warm-season transplants should go in early this week
- Succession-sow bush beans and summer squash every 2 weeks
- Harvest early tomatoes, greens, peas, and summer squash
- Deep water and heavy mulch — monitor soil moisture daily

**Pest alerts:**
- **Late blight (Phytophthora infestans):** #1 tomato threat in PA humid summers. Spreads by wind, can destroy a planting in days. Scout daily in humid weather.
- **Japanese beetles:** Copper-green adults arriving late June in large numbers. Hand-pick or use traps.
- **Squash bugs and armyworms:** Hit hardest within 3 weeks of planting. Weekly inspections required.
- **Spotted lanternfly:** Continue monitoring and destroying egg masses.

**Rotation tip:** Rotate crops to break disease cycles. Avoid planting tomatoes/peppers/eggplant where they grew last year.
