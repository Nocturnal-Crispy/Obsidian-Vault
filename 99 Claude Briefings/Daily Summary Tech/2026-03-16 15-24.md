### AI Chip Supply Crisis Deepens — CPU Demand Unprecedented
**Heat: High**
**Trend: Escalating Trend**

AMD and Intel are warning Chinese customers of CPU supply shortages with 6-month delivery lead times and 10%+ price increases. AMD's data center chief Forrest Norrod called demand increases unprecedented over the last six to nine months. NVIDIA may reduce GeForce production by 30-40% to reallocate scarce memory toward AI accelerators. TSMC reported 30% revenue growth in the first two months of 2026 driven by AI hardware demand. The AI boom has created global shortages in RAM, helium, and aluminum.

**What changed:** Supply constraints are now cascading beyond GPUs into CPUs, memory, and raw materials — a systemic bottleneck.
**Who is affected:** Every PC and server buyer, consumer GPU gamers (potential GeForce shortages), data center operators, memory manufacturers.
**What to watch:** Q2 memory pricing; NVIDIA GeForce production numbers; Intel 18A capacity ramp; whether CPU shortages trigger enterprise procurement delays.
