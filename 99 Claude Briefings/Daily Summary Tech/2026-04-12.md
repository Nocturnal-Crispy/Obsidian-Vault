# Tech Intelligence — Sunday, April 12, 2026

## 1. Iranian PLC Targeting Escalates — NERC Now Actively Monitoring Grid
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (CISA/FBI joint alert April 8), Tier 2 (Federal News Network, Utility Dive, SecurityWeek)**

The Iran-linked critical-infrastructure campaign flagged yesterday has now triggered an active NERC grid-monitoring response. Federal News Network and Utility Dive both report that NERC is coordinating with utilities on the CISA joint advisory, which identified thousands of internet-exposed Rockwell Automation PLCs being actively probed or compromised by Iranian-linked actors. The attack surface is enormous: an estimated 50-80% of US grid control endpoints rely on PLCs for substation automation, distributed energy resource management, and generation balancing. Hacks have already disrupted operations and caused financial losses at some organizations. The timing could not be worse — CISA remains at degraded capacity through the DHS shutdown (now Day 58, though employees are being recalled with back pay), and the Islamabad talks collapsed today without a deal (see Politics 04-12 Story 1), meaning the strategic motivation for Iranian cyber pressure has not diminished. This is now a confirmed, active, multi-sector campaign with grid-level implications.

## 2. Nvidia Locks Up TSMC CoWoS-L Advanced Packaging Capacity
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (CNBC April 8), Tier 2 (industry analysis)**

Nvidia has reserved the majority of TSMC's latest-generation CoWoS-L advanced packaging capacity — the critical manufacturing step that connects smaller chiplets into a single GPU package — for its Blackwell and next-generation products. Demand is so heavy that TSMC has outsourced some packaging steps to ASE and Amkor. This is the new bottleneck: advanced packaging, not wafer fabrication, is the constraint on AI GPU production in 2026. The capacity is almost entirely in Asia, though TSMC is now building its first US packaging plants. Meanwhile, Elon Musk has tapped Intel for custom chip packaging in Texas, and the broader trend of frontier labs (Anthropic, OpenAI, Meta) moving toward custom silicon is driven in part by this exact bottleneck — if you cannot buy Nvidia allocation, you build around it. This story compounds yesterday's Nvidia VP admission that even Nvidia's own research teams cannot get enough GPUs: the constraint is physical, structural, and deepening.

## 3. Anthropic MCP Crosses 97 Million Installs — De Facto Agent Protocol
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 (AI industry reporting, Crescendo AI)**

Anthropic's Model Context Protocol crossed 97 million installs in March 2026 and every major AI provider now ships MCP-compatible tooling. MCP has become the default mechanism by which AI agents connect to external tools, APIs, and data sources — effectively the HTTP of agentic AI. This is a significant structural win: while OpenAI pursues a capital-expenditure-first strategy ($600B through 2030) and Google competes on model efficiency (Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite at $0.25/M input tokens), Anthropic has quietly captured the protocol layer. Protocol dominance compounds differently than model dominance — it creates switching costs and ecosystem lock-in that persist even if a competitor releases a better model. The 97M number also indicates that the developer tooling ecosystem around Claude is now materially larger than any single model API's install base was 18 months ago.

## 4. OpenAI Passes $25B Annualized Revenue — IPO Steps Accelerating
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 2 (Multiple outlets, TechCrunch)**

OpenAI has surpassed $25 billion in annualized revenue and is reportedly taking early IPO steps for a potential late-2026 listing, compounding the UK Stargate pause signal from yesterday. The revenue figure positions OpenAI as the fastest AI company to reach this threshold, but context matters: Anthropic is approaching $19B annualized and growing roughly 3x faster. The IPO preparation is reshaping OpenAI's behavior — from the Stargate UK pause to tighter cost discipline to the increasingly public revenue claims. For the market, the question is whether OpenAI's $600B cumulative infra projection through 2030 is an asset (scale moat) or a liability (capex overhang) on a public balance sheet. The two leading frontier labs are now running fundamentally incompatible capital strategies into the same market, and the IPO will force OpenAI to pick one narrative.

## 5. DHS Employees Recalled With Back Pay — CISA Capacity Still Uncertain
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: SUSTAINED (character changing) | Source: Tier 1 (Federal News Network, Washington Times April 11)**

DHS Secretary Markwayne Mullin is recalling all furloughed DHS employees to their next scheduled shifts, and back pay covering Feb 14 through April 4 is being distributed (paychecks arriving April 10-16). The funding mechanism is Trump's executive order directing use of One Big Beautiful Bill Act funds. The shutdown technically continues at Day 58 — no legislation has passed — but the operational impact is shifting: bodies are returning, paychecks are flowing, and the constitutional question around the executive pay order remains unsettled. For CISA specifically, the recall matters because the Iran-linked PLC campaign is active now and grid monitoring requires staffed operations centers. The House returns Tuesday; Speaker Johnson has signaled acceptance of the Senate bipartisan bill (funding everything except ICE/CBP). Freedom Caucus opposition remains a credible blocker.
