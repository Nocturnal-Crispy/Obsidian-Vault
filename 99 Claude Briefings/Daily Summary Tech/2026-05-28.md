# Tech Intelligence — 2026-05-28

## AMD-Meta $60B AI Data Center Deal
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: RISING | Tier 1 — Confirmed**

AMD announced a 6-gigawatt, multi-year agreement with Meta Platforms valued at roughly $60 billion over five years. The deal will deploy custom AMD Instinct MI450 GPUs and 6th Gen EPYC "Venice" CPUs starting H2 2026. AMD's AI accelerator market share is projected to climb from ~9% (2025) to over 15% by year-end 2026. This is the largest single AI infrastructure deal to date and directly challenges NVIDIA's data center dominance.

**Strategic Impact:** Shifts the AI compute duopoly narrative. Meta diversifying away from NVIDIA signals that AMD's MI450 is production-ready at hyperscale.

## NVIDIA CUDA 13.3 Released
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Tier 1 — Confirmed**

Released May 26. Key features: CUDA Python 1.0 milestone (stable Python-native CUDA development), CUDA Tile for C++ (high-level tile-based kernel programming), CompileIQ compiler auto-tuning (up to 15% speedup on GEMM/attention kernels), Numba CUDA MLIR back-end, and C++23 support.

**Strategic Impact:** CUDA Python 1.0 lowers the barrier for AI/ML developers. Tile programming abstracts GPU complexity further.

## NVIDIA: No New Gaming GPU in 2026
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Tier 1 — Confirmed**

NVIDIA will not release a new gaming graphics chip in 2026 due to global memory shortage. All HBM capacity is being directed to AI accelerators. This marks the first time in nearly 30 years NVIDIA has gone a full year without a new gaming GPU. GPU prices continue rising with phased hikes from both NVIDIA and AMD since Q1 2026.

**Strategic Impact:** Gamers face another year on RTX 40/50 series. AI demand is cannibalizing consumer hardware roadmaps.

## Google I/O 2026 — Gemini 3.5 Flash & Ecosystem
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Tier 1 — Confirmed**

Google launched Gemini 3.5 Flash (rivals flagship models at Flash-tier speeds), Gemini Omni (multimodal video generation from any input), Gemini Spark (personal AI agent across connected apps), Universal Cart (cross-surface shopping), and Audio glasses (fall 2026).
