## 1. Iran War Day 54 — Ceasefire Extended but Iran Seizes 2 Ships in Hormuz
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: ESCALATED | Source: Tier 1 (CNN, CNBC, Washington Post, Al Jazeera, NBC, NPR, ABC)**

The ceasefire is functionally hollow — both sides are conducting escalatory actions while nominally observing it. The ship seizures are a direct challenge to the US position and create a new flashpoint. If the US responds with force to protect commercial shipping, the ceasefire collapses entirely.

## 2. DHS Shutdown Day 67 — Senate GOP Unveils $70B Immigration Plan, Employees Getting Back Pay
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (CNN, CNBC, CBS, Federal News Network, NARFE, White House)**

The DHS shutdown — now the longest government shutdown in US history at 67 days — saw two significant developments. First, the Senate Budget Committee released a $70 billion resolution to fund ICE and Border Patrol for three years (through the remainder of Trump's term), attempting to circumvent Democratic opposition through budget reconciliation. The Senate voted 52-46 to advance the process. Second, Trump issued a presidential directive ordering all DHS employees returned to paid status using available funds with "reasonable and logical nexus" to DHS functions — a legally creative maneuver. AFGE is launching nationwide airport actions to pressure resolution. House Speaker Johnson signaled he would not pass any partial DHS package until ICE/Border Patrol funding is secured. The structural deadlock continues: Democrats demand changes to immigration enforcement practices; Republicans refuse to negotiate those terms. The $70B reconciliation path is the first new procedural mechanism attempted since the shutdown began.

## 3. Israel-Lebanon 10-Day Ceasefire + Thursday Peace Talks in DC
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW (since last briefing) | Source: Tier 1 (State Dept, Al Jazeera, Times of Israel, NPR, Washington Post)**

A 10-day cessation of hostilities between Israel and Lebanon began April 16 following weeks of devastating conflict — over 2,000 killed in Lebanon, 1M+ displaced. The ceasefire was brokered by the US as a "gesture of goodwill" to enable peace talks. The first round of direct talks occurred April 14 in Washington, hosted by Secretary Rubio, with Israeli, Lebanese, and US ambassadors plus Michael Needham and UN Ambassador Waltz. A second round is scheduled for Thursday April 24. Lebanon will request a one-month ceasefire extension and an end to Israeli operations in occupied areas. The ceasefire expires around April 26 — 4 days away. Both sides have accused each other of violations. This is the most significant Israel-Lebanon diplomatic engagement since the 2006 war, but the window is extremely narrow.

## 4. Hungary — Magyar Supermajority Confirmed, Orban Era Ends
**Heat: High | Momentum: RESOLVED | Source: Tier 1 (CNN, Al Jazeera, Euronews, BBC, Chatham House)**

The April 12 election results are now fully confirmed: Peter Magyar's Tisza party won 138 of 199 seats (53.6% of the vote) — a two-thirds constitutional supermajority — ending Viktor Orban's 16-year rule. Turnout was 79.6%, a record since 1990. Orban conceded on election night. This is the largest seat share any Hungarian party has won in a free election. The supermajority gives Magyar the ability to amend Hungary's constitution, potentially reversing Orban-era institutional changes. Implications cascade through EU politics: Hungary's vetoes on Ukraine aid, migration policy, and rule-of-law enforcement are likely to shift. VP Vance's pre-election Budapest rally for Orban now stands as a notable diplomatic miscalculation.
