# Tech Intelligence — Friday, April 10, 2026

## 1. Meta–CoreWeave $21B Expansion — Rubin Ramps, Backlog Hits $66B
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Bloomberg, CNBC, CoreWeave IR)**

CoreWeave announced an expanded multi-year agreement with Meta worth approximately $21 billion, running from 2027 through December 2032. The deal lifts Meta's total CoreWeave commitment to roughly $35 billion (on top of a prior $14.2B arrangement) and pushes CoreWeave's total revenue backlog past $66 billion. Capacity will be distributed across multiple CoreWeave data center locations and will feature some of the first production deployments of Nvidia's upcoming Vera Rubin systems. CoreWeave shares jumped ~12% in early trading on the news. The strategic read: hyperscaler AI capex is now being locked in through 2032, the Rubin rollout has its anchor customer beyond Nvidia's direct supply chain, and CoreWeave is cementing its position as the de facto neutral AI cloud for companies that want Nvidia silicon without being hostage to Azure/AWS/GCP. Combined with a separate CoreWeave–Anthropic multi-year deal announced in the same 48-hour window, nine of the ten top AI model providers are now on CoreWeave's platform.

## 2. Anthropic Project Glasswing + Mythos Preview — 40+ Orgs, Thousands of Zero-Days
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (Anthropic, CrowdStrike, Fortune, TechCrunch), Tier 2 (VentureBeat, SiliconAngle)**

Project Glasswing, Anthropic's initiative to restrict Claude Mythos Preview to critical-infrastructure defenders, has escalated from announcement to operational program. Launch partners include AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft, Nvidia, and Palo Alto Networks, with access extended to 40+ additional organizations. Anthropic reports Mythos Preview has already identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities — many critical — across every major operating system and every major web browser. Anthropic is explicitly withholding the model from general release because it is also capable of exploiting those zero-days when directed. OpenAI is responding with its own "Trusted Access for Cyber" pilot, signaling that gated frontier-cyber AI models are now the new competitive arena. The dual-use implications are staggering: the same capability that lets defenders find every critical flaw in the stack lets attackers do the same if it ever leaks.

## 3. Anthropic Explores Custom AI Silicon — Breaking the Nvidia Lock
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 (Seoul Economic Daily, multiple outlets)**

Reports emerged that Anthropic is exploring the design of its own custom AI chips, joining OpenAI and Meta in the move away from pure Nvidia dependence. The strategic logic is consistent across all three: controlling the raw silicon layer is increasingly viewed as a prerequisite to leading the AI market, both for cost control and for guaranteed supply. For Anthropic, which is simultaneously signing multi-year GPU contracts with CoreWeave to power Claude at production scale, the message is split — stay on Nvidia for near-term throughput, but build optionality for 2028+. This mirrors the "vertical integration or die" pattern that defined the PC era. Tier-2 hedging: this is reporting of intent, not a taped-out chip; execution risk is high, and Anthropic has not officially confirmed.

## 4. Anthropic Growth Rate ~3x OpenAI — Gap Closes Fast
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 2 (TNW, Substack financial analysis)**

Fresh reporting tracks OpenAI at approximately $24B annualized revenue for 2026, but notes Anthropic is now growing roughly three times as fast as OpenAI. Two days ago the Anthropic revenue figure was reported at ~$19B annualized. If the 3x growth differential holds even for one more quarter, Anthropic crosses OpenAI on a run-rate basis before year-end — something that would have been unthinkable 12 months ago. Meanwhile, OpenAI introduced a new $100/month top-tier subscription offering higher limits, faster responses, and priority access to advanced models — a direct response to Claude's enterprise momentum. The efficiency and enterprise tiers are now converging into the primary battleground; the consumer chat layer is becoming a loss-leader.

## 5. Molotov Attack on Sam Altman's Home — Suspect in Custody
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Al Jazeera, CBC, Fox News, Mission Local, SFPD)**

Early Friday morning, ~4:00 a.m. PT, an incendiary device was thrown at the exterior gate of OpenAI CEO Sam Altman's San Francisco home, setting it alight. Nobody was injured. SFPD arrested a 20-year-old suspect, Daniel Alejandro Moreno-Gama, approximately an hour later near OpenAI's headquarters, where he was allegedly threatening to set the building on fire. He was booked on suspicion of attempted murder, arson, and possession/manufacture of an incendiary device. OpenAI says it is cooperating with law enforcement. The physical-security escalation for frontier AI executives is now an operational reality, not a hypothetical — a trend that has been quietly building and that will force changes in how AI companies handle leadership exposure, office access, and public engagements.

## 6. Oil — Brent Holds Near $95, Dated Brent Still Signals Physical Stress
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (CNBC, Washington Times, Trading Economics)**

Brent for June delivery eased 0.8% to $95.20/bbl on Friday; WTI May dropped 1.3% to $96.57/bbl as markets positioned ahead of the Islamabad talks. But the dated-Brent benchmark — the real physical-spot reference — rebounded over 7% from Thursday, and sits well off the record $144.42 it printed Tuesday before the ceasefire announcement. The unprecedented gap between dated Brent and front-month futures is flashing one clear signal: the paper market believes the ceasefire, the physical market does not. Only a trickle of tankers is crossing Hormuz. Goldman now warns that another month of Hormuz disruption puts Brent above $100 throughout 2026. This is the single most important price in the economy right now, and it is not stable.
