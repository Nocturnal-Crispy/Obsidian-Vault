# Agriculture Intelligence — 2026-06-10

## Livestock

### 1. Record Cattle Prices as Herd Rebuilding Stalls
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Confirmed)**

USDA projects fed-cattle prices to average a record $236/cwt in 2026, up from $224/cwt in 2025. August live cattle futures closed June 9 at $239.70, up $2.97 on the day. The composite all-fresh retail beef price hit $9.95/lb in December and has set new records every month since June 2025. Beef production is forecast to contract 2% in 2026 following a 3.5% decline in 2025.

**Herd Rebuilding Status:** After six consecutive years of contraction, the beef cow herd is estimated to begin 2026 about 150,000 head larger, near 28 million. However, meaningful rebuilding is now pushed to 2027 — lack of significant heifer retention means little or no herd growth is possible in 2026. Total red meat and poultry production for 2027 is forecast above 2026 levels only because higher pork and poultry production offsets continued beef declines.

**Strategic Impact:** Record cattle prices benefit cow-calf operators but squeeze feedlots and processors. Consumers face sustained high beef prices through at least 2027. The delayed rebuild extends the tight supply cycle.

### 2. HPAI Remains Active — 763 Outbreaks Across 31 Countries
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Confirmed)**

Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza (HPAI H5N1) continues to affect wild birds, domestic poultry, and US dairy cattle. Since April 23, 2026: 763 outbreaks/events reported in 31 countries (H5Nx: 95, H5N1: 659, H5N5: 3, H5N9: 3, H7N3: 3). APHIS has confirmed HPAI in domestic livestock in 18 states. USDA's National Milk Testing Strategy is operational for H5N1 surveillance of the dairy herd and milk supply. CDC assesses current public health risk as low but monitoring continues.

**Strategic Impact:** The dairy cattle vector remains a novel concern. Biosecurity compliance and milk testing add operational costs for dairy operations. Poultry producers face ongoing culling risk. No human-to-human transmission confirmed, but continued zoonotic monitoring is critical.

### 3. USDA Small Processors Action Plan
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Confirmed)**

Secretary Brooke Rollins launched the Small Processors Action Plan to support small meat and poultry processing plants. This continues the post-COVID push to diversify the US meat processing sector, which remains concentrated among four major packers (Tyson, JBS, Cargill, National Beef).

## Crops

### 4. WASDE: Tighter Grain Supplies for 2026/27 Marketing Year
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Confirmed)**

The May 2026 WASDE (No. 671) projects significantly tighter US and global grain supplies:

| Crop | 2026/27 Projection | Year-over-Year | Price Forecast |
|------|-------------------|----------------|----------------|
| Wheat | 1,561M bu | Down 424M bu (-21%) | $6.50/bu (+$1.50) |
| Corn | 16.0B bu | Down 6% | $4.40/bu (+$0.25) |
| Soybeans | 4.435B bu | Up 173M bu | $11.40/bu (+$1.00) |

Key driver for wheat: Winter wheat forecast down 25% to 1,048M bu, primarily from sharply reduced Hard Red Winter production. All-wheat yield projected at 47.5 bu/acre — 5.8 below last year's record. Corn ending stocks at 12.1% of use, lowest in recent averages. Soybeans expanding on higher planted area and trend yield.

**Strategic Impact:** Wheat is the headline — a 21% production drop is significant. Higher grain prices will push feed costs up, compounding pressure on livestock operators. Corn tightness supports ethanol margins but squeezes feed buyers.

## Gardening — Zone 6a/6b (Hermitage, PA)

### 6. Mid-June Seasonal Guidance
**Heat: Low | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 2 (Reputable)**

**What to do now:**
- **Direct sow:** Succession plantings of beans, cucumbers, summer squash, zucchini. Still time for a late corn planting.
- **Transplant care:** Tomatoes and peppers should be established — begin consistent watering schedule as heat increases. Mulch heavily to retain moisture.
- **Harvest:** Lettuce, peas, radishes, early strawberries, spring greens. Peas will finish soon as temperatures rise.
- **Take softwood cuttings:** Excellent time to propagate shrubs (spirea, lilac, viburnum).

**Pest Alerts for June:**
- **Squash vine borers** — Active now through August. Watch for sawdust-like frass at base of squash stems. Row covers are the best prevention.
- **Cucumber beetles** — Yellow-green with black stripes/spots. Spread bacterial wilt that can collapse plants within days. Hand-pick early; yellow sticky traps help.
- **Aphids** — Clustering on new growth tips. Reproduce rapidly. Spray off with water; ladybugs are effective biological control.
- **June bugs** — Active evenings/nights. Grubs damage turf and root crops.

**Weather Note:** Watch for possible drought conditions beginning in mid-June. Set up drip irrigation or soaker hoses now. Stay out of the garden when foliage is wet to prevent disease transmission.
