# Q1 2026 Strategic Intelligence Report

## Quarter Overview

Q1 2026 will be remembered as the quarter that redefined multiple industries simultaneously. The US-Israel war on Iran (beginning late February) triggered the most severe energy supply shock since the 1973 oil embargo, sending Brent crude from ~$75 to $119/bbl and US gas past $4/gallon. The AI infrastructure arms race accelerated to unprecedented scale with Nvidia reaching $4T+ market cap, AMD landing a $60B Meta deal, and Micron's HBM4 selling out through 2026. Anthropic leaked its "Mythos" model and prepared for a $380B IPO while OpenAI crossed $25B ARR. The DHS shutdown became the longest in US history (45+ days), gutting CISA during an active Iranian cyber campaign. The "No Kings" protests drew 8-9 million people — the largest single-day protest in American history. In gaming, an extraordinary cluster of indie and AA titles shattered Steam concurrent records.

## Industry Power Shifts

**AI Compute**: Nvidia's dominance deepened but AMD emerged as a credible challenger. The $60B Meta-AMD deal broke Nvidia's cloud monopoly assumption. Intel pivoted to collaboration (Xeon 6 in Nvidia DGX). The AI compute market is bifurcating into training (still Nvidia-dominated) and inference/agentic (increasingly competitive).

**AI Models**: The Anthropic-OpenAI rivalry intensified from competition to open conflict. Amodei labeled Altman's safety claims "straight up lies." The DOD lawsuit over Anthropic's refusal of military AI use revealed a fundamental industry schism. Claude Mythos represents a potential capabilities leap. Revenue scale reached $19-25B ARR for the top two labs.

**Energy Markets**: The Iran war created a structural energy price shock. Iran's selective Hormuz reopening for China/Russia/India created an unprecedented two-tier global shipping system that aligns maritime commerce with geopolitical blocs. This may persist beyond any ceasefire.

**Gaming Platforms**: Microsoft's multiplatform strategy accelerated (South of Midnight on PS5/Switch 2, Hades II day-one Game Pass). Sony's dynamic pricing experiment generated sustained backlash. Steam saw the strongest quarter for indie games in platform history.

## Major Technology Developments (Q1 Summary)

- **January (CES 2026)**: Nvidia, AMD, Intel battled for AI chip dominance. AMD revealed Ryzen AI 400, Turin details. Nvidia debuted new consumer and data center architectures.
- **February**: Apple-Google Siri/Gemini partnership detailed ($1B/yr). Anthropic DOD lawsuit filed. LiteLLM supply chain attack. Iran war began late February.
- **March**: Nvidia GTC (Vera Rubin production, Groq 3 LPU, inference pivot). AMD-Meta $60B deal. Micron HBM4 mass production ($23.86B quarter). Anthropic Mythos leaked. CISA gutted. FBI Director email breached. AI enterprise security crisis.

## Gaming Ecosystem Evolution (Q1 Summary)

The quarter was defined by an extraordinary indie and AA breakout on Steam:
- Hollow Knight: Silksong — 587K peak (biggest indie ever on Steam)
- Slay the Spire 2 — 574K peak (genre-defining sequel)
- ARC Raiders — 482K ATH (strongest EA run in years)
- Resident Evil Requiem — 344K ATH (franchise record)
- Bongo Cat — ~200K peak (viral rhythm phenomenon)

Platform dynamics shifted as Microsoft expanded multiplatform, Sony pushed dynamic pricing, and Game Pass continued to be the primary distribution strategy for Xbox first-party and partner titles.
