# Weekly Intelligence Rollup — Week 12 (March 16–22, 2026)

## Executive Summary

Week 12 was dominated by three converging forces: Nvidia's GTC 2026 reshaping the AI infrastructure landscape, the Iran-US conflict escalating through nuclear facility strikes and Strait of Hormuz disruption, and the Anthropic DOD lawsuit crystallizing the battle over AI ethics in military contexts. In gaming, Slay the Spire 2 set new Steam records while Crimson Desert launched strong but disappointed investors.

## Technology

### Nvidia GTC 2026 — Industry-Defining Week
The most significant hardware announcements in years:
- **Vera Rubin NVL72** now shipping to Azure — 50 petaflop GPU, ~50x more tokens/watt than Blackwell H200
- **Groq 3 LPU** (from $20B acquisition) ships Q3 2026 — 128-unit LPX rack delivers 35x throughput per megawatt
- **Feynman architecture (2028)** confirmed: 3D die stacking, custom HBM, TSMC A16 1.6nm node
- **Project Space-1** orbital data center concept announced
- **$1 trillion** in combined Blackwell + Vera Rubin purchase orders through 2027
- **DLSS 5** neural rendering announced for gaming
- **Nvidia Dynamo** software stack for inference optimization

**Week-over-week trend:** Nvidia moved from "dominant chip company" to "sole full-stack AI infrastructure provider" in a single keynote. The competitive gap with AMD widened significantly.

### Security

## Politics & Geopolitics

### Iran-US War — Week 4
The defining crisis of 2026 continued to escalate:
- **Two strikes on Natanz** nuclear facility (including second strike March 21)
- **13 US military dead**, 230+ wounded
- **$200B Pentagon war funding request** pending at White House
- **Strait of Hormuz** effectively closed — 70% tanker traffic drop, 21 ship attacks, Brent peaked $126/bbl
- **Iran retaliated** with ballistic missiles on Diego Garcia — conflict expanding beyond Middle East
- **NATO allies refused** to commit forces to Strait; Trump called them "COWARDS" and "PAPER TIGER"
- **Congress demanding exit plan** — Senate War Powers resolution defeated 47-53, but pressure mounting
- **DHS shutdown enters week 5** — Senate failed 47-37 to advance funding bill Saturday

**Week-over-week trend:** The conflict is expanding (Diego Garcia retaliation), economic costs are compounding (oil, shipping), and domestic political opposition is growing. The convergence of active war + DHS shutdown + allied non-cooperation is historically unprecedented.

### AI & Trade Policy
- **White House AI legislative framework** — federal preemption of state AI laws, developer liability limits, standardized data center permitting. Most concrete federal AI regulation step to date.
- **Anthropic DOD lawsuit** — Pentagon labeled Anthropic "supply-chain risk," then signed deal with OpenAI. Pentagon called Anthropic "unacceptable national security risk." Microsoft filed brief supporting Anthropic. 30+ OpenAI/Google employees filed cross-company support statement.
- **Section 301 investigations** expanded to 16 countries (initiated March 11) — broadest trade investigation scope in modern history
- **Democrats sharpening tariff opposition** for 2026 midterms — targeting 6 key governorships

**Week-over-week trend:** The AI policy landscape is bifurcating: companies aligned with defense get regulatory relief; those asserting ethical red lines face government retaliation. This is the most important AI governance development of 2026.
