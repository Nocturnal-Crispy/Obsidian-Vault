## US-Iran War Day 90: New Strikes as Ceasefire Frays
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 — Confirmed**

The US military carried out overnight strikes against Bandar Abbas, destroying attack drones and a ground control site that posed threats to forces and maritime traffic near the Strait of Hormuz. Hours later, Iran fired a ballistic missile at a US air base in Kuwait — intercepted by Kuwaiti forces with no casualties reported.

Both sides accuse the other of violating the ceasefire agreed three days ago. American and Iranian negotiators had reached a deal outline, but both are delaying finalization. The war, which began February 28 with US and Israeli strikes, has killed thousands and driven global energy prices sharply higher.

**Strategic Impact:** The escalation-despite-ceasefire pattern mirrors historical conflict negotiations where tactical provocations test diplomatic resolve. Energy markets and defense spending remain directly exposed.

## Trump Accounts App Launches Nationwide
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 — Confirmed**

The US Treasury launched the Trump Accounts app on all major app stores. The program will deposit $1,000 into investment accounts for eligible children (born Jan 1, 2025 – Dec 31, 2028) starting July 4. Designed in partnership with BNY Mellon and Robinhood. Nearly 6 million children have been signed up. JPMorgan Chase, Wells Fargo, Bank of America, and Visa have pledged additional contributions for employees' children.

**Strategic Impact:** Largest government-to-citizen investment program in US history. Creates a new cohort of retail investors and meaningfully expands Robinhood's user base. Policy implications for market structure and wealth inequality discourse will unfold over years.

## AI Regulation: Federal Preemption vs State Enforcement Intensifies
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 2 — Reputable**

The White House is pushing a national AI policy framework to replace the state-law patchwork, with the AG establishing an AI Litigation Task Force to challenge state laws. However, Congress has twice rejected moratorium proposals. Meanwhile, California's Transparency in Frontier AI Act and AB 2013 are in effect. The Protecting Consumers From Deceptive AI Act was introduced April 23.

State AGs are also ramping up antitrust scrutiny of AI-assisted pricing tools.

**Strategic Impact:** The federal-state tension creates compliance uncertainty for AI companies operating nationally. No resolution expected before midterms.

## Federal Judge Declines to Block Mail-in Voting Executive Order
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 — Reputable**

A federal judge in Washington, D.C. declined to temporarily block the executive order restricting mail-in voting. The decision allows the policy to remain in effect while legal challenges continue.

**Strategic Impact:** Sets the stage for continued litigation ahead of 2026 midterm elections. Voter access organizations expected to escalate legal challenges.
