# Agriculture Intelligence — 2026-06-24

## Livestock

### US Cattle Herd Shrinks to 86.2M Head — 7th Straight Year of Contraction
**Heat: High | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: SUSTAINED**

USDA outlook data shows the US cattle herd contracting to ~86.2 million head, the seventh consecutive annual decline. Tight supplies keep prices elevated: fed cattle averaging near $240/cwt and feeder calves near $364/cwt, with cattle/calf receipts projected up $5.2B (+4.1%). This extends yesterday's strong-cash-price read ($259/cwt cash).

**Why it matters:** Structural herd contraction underpins multi-year-high beef prices and supports producer margins, but signals continued tight supply and elevated consumer beef costs into 2026–2027.

**Cross-link:** Improved feed margins (see Crops) may slow further liquidation.

Sources: [USDA ERS — Farm Income Forecast](https://www.ers.usda.gov/topics/farm-economy/farm-sector-income-finances/farm-sector-income-forecast), [DTN/Progressive Farmer](https://www.dtnpf.com/agriculture/web/ag/livestock/article/2026/02/19/livestock-poultry-prices-expected)

### Egg Production Rebounds 6%; K-State H5N1 Vaccine Push
**Heat: Medium | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: RISING**

US egg production is projected to rise 6% to 9.2 billion dozen in 2026, recovering from a 4% HPAI-driven drop in 2025 (8.65B dozen). Researchers, partnering with Kansas State University, are investing $400K+ into H5N1 vaccine development. Since March 2024 USDA has confirmed 1,000+ H5N1 cases in cattle. CDC continues to rate human risk as low.

**Why it matters:** Egg-supply recovery should ease price pressure, but H5N1 remains an active background risk into fall migration. Vaccine progress is the key medium-term variable for poultry and dairy.

Sources: [USDA WASDE](https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/general-information/staff-offices/office-chief-economist/commodity-markets/wasde-report), [Food Tank](https://foodtank.com/news/2026/03/food-tanks-weekly-news-roundup-snap-usda-avian-flu-fertilizer-prices/), [CDC Bird Flu](https://www.cdc.gov/bird-flu/situation-summary/index.html)

## Crops

### Lower Corn & Soybean-Meal Prices Improve Feeding Margins
**Heat: Medium | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: NEW**

USDA projects the 2026/27 US corn crop at ~15.8 billion bushels (ending stocks 1.84B) and soybeans at 4.45B bushels (ending stocks ~355M). The 2026/27 season-average soybean price is forecast at $11.40/bu (vs $10.40 in 2025/26). Lower corn and soybean-meal prices are improving livestock feeding margins.

**Why it matters:** Cheaper feed eases pressure on cattle and hog feeders — a counterweight to last week's tighter-corn read and a factor that could slow herd liquidation.

**Cross-link:** Directly supports the Livestock margin picture above.

Sources: [USDA WASDE](https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/general-information/staff-offices/office-chief-economist/commodity-markets/wasde-report), [USDA ERS](https://www.ers.usda.gov/topics/farm-economy/farm-sector-income-finances/farm-sector-income-forecast)

### NASS Reschedules Reports Around Government Closure
**Heat: Low | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: NEW**

USDA NASS issued a notice (June 22) rescheduling certain reports around a government-closure period. Producers and traders should confirm release dates for upcoming crop-progress and stocks reports to avoid acting on stale calendars.

**Why it matters:** Report-timing shifts can move thin summer markets; worth noting for anyone tracking grain basis or futures around release dates.

Source: [USDA NASS Notice](https://www.nass.usda.gov/Newsroom/Notices/2026/06-22-2026.php)

## Gardening (Hermitage, PA — Zone 6a/6b)

### Late-June Window: Dry, Sunny Today; Storms Thursday
**Heat: Low | Tier 2 — Reputable | Momentum: NEW**

Today is sunny, high ~78°F, near-calm winds — an excellent window for transplanting, succession sowing (beans, summer squash, cucumbers, fall brassica starts), weeding, and harvest. Thursday brings 80% chance of showers/thunderstorms, so finish outdoor work and any spraying today. No frost risk — well into the growing season. Watch for spotted lanternfly and squash vine borer pressure typical of late June in western PA.

**Why it matters:** Today is the best dry-work window of the next several days before a wet Thursday. Time transplanting and any foliar applications accordingly.
