# Gaming Intelligence — Thursday, April 23, 2026

## 1. Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced — Worldwide Reveal Showcase Today
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Ubisoft News, YouTube), Tier 2 (Game Informer, TechRadar, TweakTown)**

Ubisoft's dedicated worldwide reveal showcase for Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced airs today at 9:00 AM PDT / 6:00 PM CEST. The game targets a July 9, 2026 release with native 4K/60fps and seamless ship-to-shore transitions. Key details from pre-reveal reporting: the remake retains the open-world Caribbean setting and Edward Kenway as protagonist but removes multiplayer and legacy DLC, focusing on an expanded single-player campaign. Crucially, it will NOT be an RPG — it preserves the classic AC structure. This is Ubisoft's biggest bet of the year. The timing is deliberate — revealing between the Game Pass restructuring news and Diablo IV's expansion launch, maximizing visibility during a high-attention window.

## 2. Diablo IV: Lord of Hatred — 5 Days Out, Launch Trailer Drops
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (Blizzard, Steam, Battle.net), Tier 2 (PC Gamer, RPG Site)**

Blizzard released the Lord of Hatred launch trailer with the expansion now 5 days from release (April 28). Pre-purchases continue to grant instant Paladin class access and Vessel of Hatred expansion. PC Gamer's early review is positive. The two-class addition (Paladin and Warlock) plus new Skovos region makes this the largest single expansion in Diablo franchise history. The ARPG competitive landscape is intense — Path of Exile 2 continues to outperform Diablo IV in concurrent counts since going premium. Lord of Hatred is Blizzard's chance to close that gap.

## 3. Game Pass Wave 2 April: Kiln Today, Heroes of Might & Magic, Final Fantasy V
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Xbox Wire)**

Game Pass Wave 2 for April adds 10 titles between April 21 and May 5. Today's addition: **Kiln** (pottery-themed party brawler). Notable upcoming additions include **Trepang2** (April 29), **Heroes of Might & Magic: Olden Era** (April 30), and **Final Fantasy V** (May 5). The lineup is catalog-depth rather than headline-grabbing — consistent with Microsoft's new post-COD-removal strategy of emphasizing value through volume and variety rather than day-one blockbusters.
