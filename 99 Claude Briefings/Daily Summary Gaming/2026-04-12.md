# Gaming Intelligence — Sunday, April 12, 2026

## 1. Hades II Console Launch — 2 Days Out, Final Pre-Launch Window
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (Supergiant, Xbox Wire, GameSpot, Kotaku)**

Hades II ships Tuesday April 14 on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and Cloud — day-one on Game Pass Ultimate, Premium, and PC Game Pass. A general update with new bonus content and quality-of-life improvements drops simultaneously across all platforms including PC and Switch. Steam concurrency remains in the pre-console trough (~8K peak vs 112.9K all-time), which is expected: the console/Game Pass onramp is the catalyst. The key metrics on launch day are (1) whether combined PC + Game Pass CCU exceeds the original 112K peak, confirming Game Pass pull-in, and (2) whether Twitch viewership spikes correlate with Game Pass trial conversions. REPLACED also ships Tuesday as a day-one Game Pass title, testing whether a double-A launch can hold attention alongside a flagship. This is the single biggest Game Pass day of 2026 so far.

## 2. Pragmata — 5 Days Out, Switch 2's First Real AAA Test
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (Capcom), Tier 2 (PC Gamer, TweakTown)**

Pragmata launches April 17 on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC (Steam), and Nintendo Switch 2 (Japan/Asia get Switch 2 version April 24). GeForce Now is adding Pragmata to its cloud library at launch. Capcom moved the date forward a week on confidence — meaningful from a publisher that delayed this title multiple times during its five-year development. The ~2M pre-release demo downloads and consistently positive extended previews position this as Capcom's biggest new-IP swing since Dragon's Dogma 2. For Switch 2, this is the clearest test of whether third-party publishers will treat the platform as launch-day parity rather than a delayed port target. Post-Monster Hunter Wilds and RE Requiem, Pragmata is Capcom's 2026 new-IP verdict.

## 3. Game Pass April — Strongest Single-Month Lineup Since Late 2024
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Xbox Wire, GameSpot, Kotaku)**

The April Game Pass catalog now totals 17+ titles across two waves, with five day-one additions. Wave 1 (live): Planet Coaster 2, Tiny Bookshop. Wave 2 (coming): Oblivion Remastered, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare (2019), Kiln, Vampire Crawlers, and more. The Tuesday double-drop of Hades II + REPLACED anchors the month. Game Pass continues to widen the perceived value gap versus PlayStation Plus, which has still not responded publicly to the dynamic-pricing backlash from late March. The density play is deliberate: Microsoft is using April as a proof-of-concept month for "catalog as moat" ahead of the E3-replacement summer showcase window.

## 4. Steam Weekend — Holding Pattern Before the April 14/17 Stack
**Heat: Low | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Steam, SteamDB), Tier 3 (community)**

Steam charts remain structurally unchanged: Counter-Strike 2 at #1 (~763K avg 30-day), Dota 2 #2, PUBG and ARC Raiders (~400K daily avg) rounding out the top tier. No new indie breakouts this weekend. The April 11-12 window is a classic pre-release holding pattern — players are clearing backlogs ahead of Tuesday/Thursday drops. Path of Exile 2 continues to outperform Diablo IV and Last Epoch in concurrent counts since going premium. Hollow Knight Silksong and Slay the Spire 2 maintain healthy long-tail residuals off March peaks.
