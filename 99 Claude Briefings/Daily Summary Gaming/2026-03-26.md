# Gaming Intelligence — Thursday, March 26, 2026

## Story 1: Life is Strange: Reunion — Launch Day
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

The most anticipated narrative game of Q1 2026 is live. Life is Strange: Reunion launches today on PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X|S — the full game, not episodic. Both Max and Chloe are playable with their signature abilities. Pre-release hands-on coverage from IGN and Polygon was overwhelmingly positive, calling it one of the franchise's strongest entries.

Notable: no pre-release reviews were provided to press, with the developer citing spoiler protection. Early physical copies have been circulating. The review embargo lifting today will be the first real signal on critical reception.

**Why it matters:** This is the biggest narrative game launch of 2026 so far. The no-review-embargo approach is a gamble — it protects story surprises but creates uncertainty. Watch for review scores and Steam concurrent player numbers through today and tomorrow.

## Story 3: Super Mario Bros. Wonder — Switch 2 Edition Launch
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

The enhanced Switch 2 version of Super Mario Bros. Wonder launches today with the Bellabel Park expansion (17 new minigames), Rosalina as a playable character, and 12-player online support. It was #2 on Switch 2 eShop pre-loads behind Pokemon Pokopia.

## Story 4: Bongo Cat — Holding at 170K, Defying Meme-Game Crash Pattern
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

Bongo Cat continues to maintain 24-hour peaks around 170-180K concurrent players, with an all-time peak of 194K. The free-to-play meme rhythm game has now sustained above 100K for over a week, which is unusual for viral novelty titles. The Banana Game and Egg precedents from 2024-2025 both crashed within days.
