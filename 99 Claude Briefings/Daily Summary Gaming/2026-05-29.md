## 1. Destiny 2 Enters Maintenance Mode — Monument of Triumph Launches June 9
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (Bungie blog, Kotaku, Massively OP)

Bungie confirmed that Destiny 2's active development is ending after nearly nine years. The final content update, Monument of Triumph, arrives June 9, 2026. Servers remain online indefinitely, but no further expansions, seasons, or content drops will follow.

Monument of Triumph includes content originally intended for future releases: the return of the Director screen (replacing the controversial Portal from July 2025), permanent Sparrow Racing League, expanded Pantheon with new boss challenges, and a Collection bundle packaging all Destiny 2 content. Bungie described the update as "a collection of love letters to players across all activity types."

**Strategic Impact:** This is the end of one of gaming's longest-running live-service franchises. Bungie (owned by Sony) can now focus resources on Marathon and future projects. The Destiny 2 player base — still substantial — will need to find new live-service homes, potentially benefiting competitors.

## 2. Embracer Group Splits: Fellowship Entertainment Takes Lord of the Rings, Tomb Raider
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (Embracer, Deadline, Variety)

Embracer Group announced a corporate split on May 20, spinning off its premium IP portfolio into Fellowship Entertainment, targeting a Nasdaq Stockholm listing in 2027. Fellowship takes Lord of the Rings, The Hobbit, Tomb Raider, Dead Island, Kingdom Come Deliverance, Metro, Remnant, and Darksiders.

A new Lord of the Rings RPG was announced alongside the split. CEO Phil Rogers and COO Lee Guinchard move to Fellowship. Chair Lars Wingefors called the Fellowship assets "among the most undervalued in the industry."

**Strategic Impact:** This restructuring gives Embracer's premium IPs dedicated leadership and investment focus. The LotR RPG announcement signals intent to compete in the AAA RPG space. Watch for acquisition interest in Fellowship once publicly listed.

## 4. Dead by Daylight 10th Anniversary: Jason Voorhees Arrives June 16
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (Behaviour Interactive, multiple outlets)

After a decade of fan requests, Jason Voorhees officially joins Dead by Daylight on June 16 as the game's 10th anniversary killer. Players on Steam PTB can test Jason from May 26.

Abilities include Omnipresent Evil (stealth/tracking — disappears from the map entirely) and Improvised Carnage (throwable debris that staggers survivors). Four cosmetic outfits launch alongside, including the classic hockey mask.

**Strategic Impact:** This is a significant cultural moment for the horror gaming community. Jason has been the most-requested character since DbD's 2016 launch. Expect a player count spike and potential re-engagement from lapsed players.

## 5. Steam Deck Prices Spike Up to 50% — Memory Shortage Hits Gaming Hardware
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (Valve, Tom's Hardware, The Register)

Valve hiked Steam Deck OLED prices by up to $300: 1TB now $949 (was $649), 512GB now $789 (was $549). The cause is the global memory shortage driven by AI infrastructure demand absorbing DRAM and NAND supply. Refurbished LCD models remain at $359.

Nintendo has also raised prices in 2026. Nvidia will not release a new gaming GPU this year — the first time in nearly 30 years — prioritizing limited memory for AI accelerators.

**Strategic Impact:** The AI compute boom is directly taxing the gaming hardware ecosystem. Handheld and console pricing is becoming a barrier for budget-conscious gamers. The refurb/used market will grow in importance.
