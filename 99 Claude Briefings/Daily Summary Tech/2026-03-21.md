# Tech Intelligence — Saturday, March 21, 2026

## Story 1: Nvidia GTC 2026 — Vera Rubin Ships, Feynman Roadmap Revealed
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)
**Categories:** GPU / Semiconductor, AI

Nvidia's GTC 2026 keynote delivered the most significant hardware roadmap update in years. The Vera Rubin NVL72 platform — a full-stack system with seven chips, five rack-scale configurations, and one supercomputer design — is now shipping to Azure as the first hyperscale deployment. Vera Rubin delivers approximately 50x more tokens per watt than Blackwell H200.

Jensen Huang also unveiled the Groq 3 Language Processing Unit, the first chip from Nvidia's $20B Groq acquisition (completed December 2025). The Groq 3 LPU targets inference acceleration and ships Q3 2026. Combined with Nvidia Dynamo, it delivers 35x more throughput per megawatt.

The Feynman architecture (2028) was confirmed with three headline innovations: 3D die stacking (first stacked GPU dies in Nvidia silicon), custom HBM memory (likely proprietary HBM4E/HBM5), and fabrication on TSMC's A16 1.6nm node — Nvidia's first sub-2nm chip. The companion CPU, Rosa (named for Rosalind Franklin), was also announced.

Headline number: $1 trillion in combined Blackwell and Vera Rubin purchase orders through 2027.

**Why it matters:** Nvidia is consolidating its position as the sole full-stack AI infrastructure provider — chips, networking, software, and now inference acceleration via Groq. The Feynman roadmap signals that Nvidia's two-year cadence is accelerating, not slowing.

**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)
**Categories:** Security
