## Livestock

### 1. HPAI Remains Widespread; Polar Bear Detection on Svalbard
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (APHIS, CDC, WOAH)

H5N1 HPAI continues across wild birds worldwide, with ongoing outbreaks in US poultry and dairy cattle (confirmed in 18+ states). The CDC maintains a "low" public health risk assessment but is actively monitoring workers with animal exposures.

In a notable development, HPAI was detected in a polar bear on Svalbard (Norway) in May 2026 — the first confirmed case in Europe's arctic mammals, following detections in walruses (2023) and arctic foxes (2025). Globally, 1,479 outbreaks across 42 countries reported as of late February.

**Strategic Impact:** The arctic mammalian spread signals continuing viral adaptation. Biosecurity remains critical for poultry and dairy operations. No new human case clusters, but surveillance posture is elevated.

### 2. Beef Production Forecast Lower as Herd Rebuilding Limits Supply
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA WASDE-671)

The May WASDE projects beef production lower in both 2026 and 2027 as cattle herd rebuilding limits fed cattle supply. Pork and broiler production are forecast higher, partially offsetting beef tightness.

**Strategic Impact:** Tight cattle supply supports elevated live and feeder cattle prices. Herd rebuild is a multi-year cycle — expect sustained price support through 2027.

## Crops

### 3. USDA WASDE May 2026: Grain Supplies Tighten for 2026/27
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA WASDE-671)

Key projections from the May WASDE:
- **Wheat:** 2026/27 production at 1,561M bushels, down 424M YoY. Both harvested area and yield declining.
- **Corn:** 2026/27 production at 16.0B bushels, down 6% YoY. Planted area declining to 95.3M acres, yield projected at 183.0 bu/acre.
- **Soybeans:** Expanding acreage partially offsets corn/wheat declines.
- **Price forecasts:** Increases across all major row crops.

**Strategic Impact:** Tighter US and global grain stocks support higher commodity prices. Feed cost pressure on livestock producers intensifies.

### 4. Drought Covers 52% of US; Trade Uncertainty Pressures Corn
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (Drought.gov, USDA), Tier 2 (Market reporting)

As of May 19, 52.15% of the US and Puerto Rico is in drought. Corn futures pulled back to ~$4.60/bu from recent one-year highs amid doubts over China's commitment to US agricultural purchases — China's Commerce Ministry clarified the $17B annual purchase figure was a "guiding target" only. Soybeans at 1,194 USD/bu, up 0.75%.

**Strategic Impact:** Drought pressure on yields combined with trade uncertainty creates a volatile pricing environment. The US-China ag purchase question is the key demand variable.

## Gardening

### 5. Zone 6a/6b (Hermitage, PA): Late May Planting Window
**Heat:** Low | **Momentum:** SEASONAL | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Old Farmer's Almanac, Penn State Extension)

Last frost dates have passed for zone 6. Current planting guidance:
- **Direct sow now:** beans, corn, cucumbers, squash, melons, radishes, baby carrots, quick-maturing salad mixes
- **Transplant now:** tomatoes, peppers, eggplant (harden off first)
- **Trees/shrubs:** Ideal planting window — cool nights, warm days help establishment
- **Perennials:** Asters, beebalm, hostas, irises, salvia performing well
- **Watch for:** Late spring frost risk in higher elevation areas; spotted lanternfly and squash vine borer season beginning

**Strategic Impact:** Peak warm-season planting window is open. Monitor weather for any anomalous cold snaps. Grassland CRP signup deadline is May 29 — tomorrow.
