## Story 2: DHS Shutdown Breakthrough — Senate Reaches Deal, Heads to House
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** ESCALATED | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

After 42 days, the DHS shutdown may be ending. In the early hours of Friday morning, the Senate moved to fund most of DHS — including TSA, Coast Guard, and most agencies — while carving out ICE and part of CBP from the deal. Democrats insisted on separating immigration enforcement funding from operational agencies as their price for agreement.

**Trump's dual moves:** The president announced an executive order directing DHS to "immediately pay" TSA agents using funding from last year's reconciliation bill ("One Big, Beautiful Bill"). He also declared an airport emergency. It remains unclear whether this funding mechanism is legally viable, but it provided political cover for the deal.

**TSA damage assessment:** 61,000 TSA employees have been working without pay since February 14. Over 480 have quit outright. Callout rates reached 40-50% at some airports. Wait times hit all-time highs — some exceeding four and a half hours. Even with funding restored, the workforce damage will take months to repair.

**Next steps:** The House can vote as early as today (Friday). The ICE/CBP carve-out means immigration enforcement funding remains unresolved, setting up a second fight.

**Why it matters:** This is the most significant development in the shutdown saga. The deal structure — fund operational agencies, isolate immigration enforcement — is a template that could resolve the immediate crisis while preserving both parties' political positioning. But the TSA workforce damage is already done: 480+ officers who quit won't return, and the retraining pipeline is slow. The ICE funding fight will likely extend through April.
