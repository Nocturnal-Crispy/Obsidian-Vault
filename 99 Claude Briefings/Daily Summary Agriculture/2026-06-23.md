# Agriculture Intelligence — 2026-06-23

## Livestock

### Live Cattle Prices Hold Strong at $259/cwt
**Heat: Medium | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: SUSTAINED**

Cash cattle prices remain elevated across major markets: Texas $259.07, Kansas $259.45, Iowa $259.17, Nebraska $259.78. Futures opened the week at steep discounts to cash, with early-week cash trade at $252 live and $395-396 dressed in the north. Today's slaughter estimate: 106,000 head (vs 99,000 last week). Boxed beef slightly lower: Choice at $389.18 (-$0.38), Select at $387.29 (-$1.31).

USDA's 2026 outlook: slaughter steer prices raised to $250.16/cwt, feeder steers lowered to $375.22/cwt.

**Why it matters:** Sustained high cash prices with futures at steep discounts suggest the market expects some correction, but tight supply fundamentals continue supporting the cash market.

Sources: [Barchart](https://www.barchart.com/futures/quotes/LEM26), [USDA ERS](https://www.ers.usda.gov/publications/115035)

### USDA Launches Small Processors Action Plan
**Heat: Medium | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: NEW**

Secretary Rollins launched the Small Processors Action Plan on June 3, a set of actions to support small and very small meat and poultry processing plants, improve customer service, and reduce unnecessary regulatory burdens. This addresses a chronic bottleneck in the US meat supply chain — processing capacity concentration.

Source: [USDA](https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/news/press-releases)

### New World Screwworm Preparedness Advisor Appointed
**Heat: Low | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: NEW**

Secretary Rollins announced on June 8 that President Trump appointed John Bellinger as Senior Advisor for New World Screwworm Preparedness. This follows ongoing concerns about screwworm reintroduction risk to US livestock.

Source: [USDA](https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/news/press-releases)

### HPAI (Avian Flu) — Ongoing in Europe, US Monitoring
**Heat: Medium | Tier 1/2 — Confirmed/Reputable | Momentum: SUSTAINED**

Europe has recorded 2,562 HPAI outbreaks across 31 states in 2026 (as of June 17), concentrated in northern/western Europe (Belgium, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Netherlands, Norway). Most recent commercial outbreak: H5N1 at a mallard duck farm in Helsingborg, Sweden. In the US, HPAI remains widespread in wild birds with sporadic cases in dairy cattle and poultry workers. CDC rates current public health risk as low.

**Cross-link:** Continued HPAI pressure supports elevated poultry prices and could tighten egg supply if US outbreaks increase during fall migration.

Sources: [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/bird-flu/situation-summary/index.html), [APHIS](https://www.aphis.usda.gov/livestock-poultry-disease/avian/avian-influenza), [WATTPoultry](https://www.wattagnet.com/poultry-meat/diseases-health/avian-influenza/news/15828015/new-avian-flu-cases-in-commercial-poultry-in-one-european-state)

## Crops

### US Corn Crop Projected Down 6% — Tighter Stocks Ahead
**Heat: High | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: NEW**

The 2026/27 US corn outlook from USDA's June WASDE shows supply, total use, and ending stocks all reduced with higher expected prices. Corn crop projected at 16.0 billion bushels, down 6% from last year on declines to both area and yield. This tightening supports higher corn futures and raises feed cost concerns for the livestock sector.

**Cross-link:** Higher corn prices directly pressure cattle and hog feeding margins, potentially accelerating herd liquidation in the back half of the year.

Source: [USDA WASDE](https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/general-information/staff-offices/office-chief-economist/commodity-markets/wasde-report)

### Crop Conditions and Progress — Mostly Favorable
**Heat: Medium | Tier 1 — Confirmed | Momentum: SUSTAINED**

USDA Crop Progress (week of June 22):
- **Corn:** 69% good-to-excellent, 4% poor-to-very-poor. Emerged 76% (vs 5-yr avg 74%). Planted 86% (vs 5-yr avg 83%).
- **Soybeans:** 67% good-to-excellent, 5% poor-to-very-poor. Emerged 65% (vs 5-yr avg 57%). Planted 87% (vs 5-yr avg 80%).

Both crops tracking at or above 5-year averages for planting and emergence, though the lower corn acreage and yield projections remain the key supply concern.

Source: [USDA NASS](https://www.nass.usda.gov/Publications/)

## Gardening — Zone 6a/6b (Hermitage, PA)

### Late June Seasonal Guidance

**Plant now:**
- Direct sow succession plantings of bush beans, cucumbers, and summer squash
- Plant sweet potato slips (soil is fully warm)
- Direct sow sunflowers, nasturtiums, marigolds, borage, basil
- Continue sowing warm-season herbs

**Start indoors now:**
- Fall cole crops (broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage) for transplanting in late July/August

**Harvest window:**
- Early plantings of lettuce, peas, radishes should be finishing — harvest before they bolt in the heat
- First harvests of snap beans, early tomatoes approaching

**Watch for:**
- Spotted lanternfly activity increasing — inspect and destroy egg masses
- Squash vine borer adults active now — row cover protection or Bt injections
- Tomato blight pressure rises with summer humidity — maintain airflow, mulch, avoid overhead watering

**Weather note:** Late June in zone 6a/6b typically sees last frost well behind, with growing season extending through early October. Focus shifts to pest management and heat stress mitigation.

Sources: [Old Farmer's Almanac PA](https://www.almanac.com/gardening/planting-calendar/PA), [Sow True Seed Zone 6](https://sowtrueseed.com/blogs/monthly-garden-schedule-by-zone/zone-6-monthly-garden-calendar-chores-and-planting-guide)
