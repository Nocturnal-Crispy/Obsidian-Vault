## Livestock

### 1. Cattle Futures Slide as Herd Rebuild Limits Supply
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (CME, USDA WASDE-671)

Live cattle futures fell $0.42 to $1.15 on Thursday, with Jun 26 closing at $250.275. Cash trade was nearly frozen as packers bid $253 against $260 asks. The CME Feeder Cattle Index dropped $4.23 to $367.26 on May 25. Falling box prices are widening the packer-producer gap.

The WASDE continues to project lower beef production in 2026 and 2027 as herd rebuilding limits fed cattle supply. Pork and broiler production are forecast higher, partially offsetting beef tightness. The all-milk price for 2027 is forecast at $20.95/cwt.

**Strategic Impact:** Cash trade gridlock signals short-term price discovery problems, but the structural story remains: tight cattle supply supports elevated prices through 2027. Feed cost pressure from grain markets adds margin risk.

**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (APHIS, CDC)

H5N1 HPAI continues across US poultry and dairy cattle (18+ states). CDC maintains "low" public health risk assessment. APHIS surveillance active across commercial, backyard, and wild birds. No new human case clusters.

## Crops

### 3. Drought Intensifies: 62% of Lower 48 in Drought; Wheat Tour Shows Devastation
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1 (Drought.gov, USDA, Wheat Quality Council)

Drought now covers 62.42% of the Lower 48 states, with the Southeast at 99% coverage (61.5% extreme/exceptional). The 2026 Wheat Quality Council Tour found devastating conditions:
- Kansas winter wheat: 93% headed but 58% rated poor to very poor
- Oklahoma wheat: 94% headed, 48% poor to very poor
- Cracked soil and premature maturation across KS, OK, CO, NE

The USDA Weather and Crop Bulletin (May 27) confirms drought persisting across the West, High Plains, South/Southeast, and spreading into Midwest and Mid-Atlantic. This is being described as the worst spring drought on record.

Corn and soybean planting is ahead of the five-year average, but yield risk is rising as drought expands.

**Strategic Impact:** Wheat production estimates may need further downward revision. The WASDE already projected 2026/27 wheat production down 424M bushels YoY. Drought pressure on corn yields could tighten stocks further if conditions persist through pollination season (late June-July). This is the agriculture story to watch.

## Gardening

### 5. Zone 6a/6b (Hermitage, PA): Peak Warm-Season Window Open
**Heat:** Low | **Momentum:** SEASONAL | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Penn State Extension, USDA)

Late May guidance for zone 6:
- **Direct sow now:** beans, corn, cucumbers, squash, melons, successive lettuce sowings
- **Transplant now:** tomatoes, peppers, eggplant should be in ground; harden off if not already
- **Maintenance:** Mulch beds to conserve moisture (drought watch), side-dress corn and heavy feeders
- **Pest watch:** Spotted lanternfly nymphs active in PA — check tree of heaven and grapevines. Squash vine borer adults emerging; row cover vulnerable plantings. Japanese beetle season approaching.
- **Perennials:** Good establishment window for trees, shrubs, and perennials with warm days and cool nights

**Strategic Impact:** Drought conditions in the broader region warrant extra attention to irrigation and mulching for home gardens. SLF remains under quarantine in most PA counties — report sightings to 1-888-4BADFLY.
