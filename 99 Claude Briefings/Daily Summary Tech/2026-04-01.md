# Tech Intelligence — Wednesday, April 1, 2026

## 1. OpenAI Closes Record $122B Funding Round at $852B Valuation
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Bloomberg, CNBC, CoinDesk)**

OpenAI completed its record-shattering $122 billion funding round on March 31, valuing the company at $852 billion. Amazon committed $50 billion, while Nvidia and SoftBank each invested $30 billion. SoftBank's second $10 billion tranche arrived today (April 1), confirming the funding cadence remains on track — the remaining tranches are due July 1 and October 1. OpenAI has crossed $2 billion/month in revenue ($25B+ annualized). The round is the largest private funding in history and positions OpenAI for a potential IPO. This fundamentally reshapes the AI competitive landscape — no other lab has anything close to this capital base. Anthropic's $380B IPO valuation (Q4 2026 target) and $19B ARR now represent a clear #2 position rather than a peer challenger.

## 5. CISA Mandates Citrix NetScaler Emergency Patch — CVE-2026-3055
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (CISA, Dataconomy)**

CISA issued a mandatory directive requiring all federal agencies to patch Citrix NetScaler appliances by April 2 due to actively exploited CVE-2026-3055. The vulnerability allows unauthenticated remote attackers to steal sensitive information from Citrix ADC or Gateway appliances configured as SAML identity providers. This comes as CISA operates with 60% furloughed staff and skipped RSA Conference 2026 entirely — the agency's reduced capacity makes even routine emergency directives harder to enforce. FBI and NSA also did not attend RSA, fueling concerns about degraded public-private cyber coordination.
