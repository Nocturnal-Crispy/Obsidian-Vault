## Technology — Week in Review

### AI Infrastructure Race Enters New Phase
The week confirmed that the 2026 AI compute shortage is structural, not cyclical. Nvidia's VP of Applied Deep Learning Research publicly stated even Nvidia's internal teams cannot get enough GPUs (April 11). Nvidia then locked up the majority of TSMC's CoWoS-L advanced packaging capacity (April 8-12), making packaging — not wafer fabrication — the new production bottleneck. TSMC outsourced steps to ASE and Amkor and is building its first US packaging plants. This structural constraint is driving all frontier labs toward custom silicon as a necessity, not a hedge.

### Anthropic vs OpenAI: Diverging Strategies
Two incompatible capital strategies are now running in parallel. OpenAI crossed $25B annualized revenue, paused its UK Stargate data center build, and is actively preparing for a late-2026 IPO — projecting $600B cumulative infra spend through 2030. Anthropic is approaching $19B revenue, growing roughly 3x faster, and captured the protocol layer with MCP hitting 97M installs. OpenAI is running a capital-first strategy; Anthropic is running a protocol-and-revenue-compounding strategy. Google entered with Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite — competing on efficiency ($0.25/M input tokens, 2.5x faster).

### Meta-CoreWeave $21B Expansion
The Meta-CoreWeave $21B infrastructure deal (2027-2032) held through the week, confirming CoreWeave's position as the neutral GPU toll road. Nvidia's $66B+ Vera Rubin backlog remains the anchor for next-generation supply.

### Cybersecurity Escalation
The Iranian-linked PLC targeting campaign escalated all week. CISA/FBI issued a joint alert April 8 identifying thousands of internet-exposed Rockwell Automation PLCs under active attack. By Saturday, NERC was actively monitoring the grid. 50-80% of US grid control endpoints rely on PLCs. CISA operated at degraded capacity through the DHS shutdown (now Day 58, though employees are being recalled). The WordPress Smart Slider 3 Pro supply-chain compromise (April 7, 6-hour poisoned window) and Citizen Lab's ad-data surveillance report (500M devices tracked by law enforcement via ad exchanges) added to the week's threat surface.

### Anthropic Project Glasswing
Project Glasswing — Anthropic's AI-powered cyber defense program — continued its rollout to 40+ partner organizations. With the Iranian PLC campaign active and CISA at reduced capacity, Glasswing's operational readiness is being tested in real time.

## Politics — Week in Review

### Islamabad Talks: From Hope to Collapse
The week opened with rising anticipation for the first direct US-Iran talks since 1979. VP Vance led a ~300-member US delegation; Iran sent ~70 members led by Parliament Speaker Ghalibaf and FM Araghchi. Pakistan's PM Sharif personally mediated. After a 5-hour delay Saturday, talks ran for 21 continuous hours before collapsing Sunday. The nuclear impasse proved insurmountable: the US required an affirmative commitment that Iran would not seek nuclear weapons; Iran maintained its sovereign right to civilian enrichment. Secondary impasses: Hormuz control, war reparations, frozen assets, Lebanon ceasefire inclusion. Vance left a "final and best offer" on the table. The 14-day ceasefire expires ~April 21 with no extension mechanism.

### Strait of Hormuz: Crisis Deepening
The Strait remained effectively closed all week. The IRGC manages transit; only one oil-products tanker transited in the first 24 hours after ceasefire. ~13M bbl/day shut in. Dated Brent hit ~$132/bbl (physical) vs ~$96/bbl (futures) — the widest physical-paper gap in modern history. Goldman warned $100+ Brent for all of 2026 if closure persists another month. The EIA raised its 2026 forecast to $96/bbl average. After Islamabad's failure, expect Monday repricing.

### Lebanon: Active Escalation
Israel expanded Lebanon operations all week. The April 8 "Black Wednesday" wave (50 jets, ~160 munitions, ~357 killed) was followed by continued daily strikes including emergency worker casualties Saturday. Five IDF divisions now deployed in southern Lebanon. Israel explicitly refused to discuss a Hezbollah ceasefire. The Lebanon question was Iran's key precondition at Islamabad and a structural reason the talks failed. With no deal, the constraint on Israeli operations has weakened further.

### Hungary Election: Decision Day
Polls showed Tisza leading Fidesz by 7-13 points all week. Sunday voting saw record 54% turnout by 11 AM (~997K more voters than 2022). VP Vance's Tuesday Budapest rally for Orban ("we have got to get Viktor Orban reelected") correlated with continued Fidesz polling declines. Preliminary results expected tonight. If Orban loses, it ends the longest-running MAGA-aligned government in Europe.

### DHS Shutdown: Week 8
Day 52-58 through the week. The Senate bipartisan bill (funding everything except ICE/CBP) awaits House action. Speaker Johnson signaled acceptance; Freedom Caucus remains a blocker. House on Easter recess until Tuesday. Trump's executive order funded back pay (Feb 14 - April 4) and Secretary Mullin recalled all furloughed employees. CISA operational capacity improving but still under strain during the Iranian cyber campaign.

### Pakistan's Diplomatic Win
Regardless of the talks' failure, Pakistan captured a durable soft-power win. The Vance visit was the most senior US engagement since 2011. Multiple allied leaders credited Islamabad's diplomacy. Watch for quiet trade, IMF, and security assistance channels reopening.
