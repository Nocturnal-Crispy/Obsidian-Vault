## 2. Supreme Court Curtails IEEPA Tariff Authority
**Heat: High | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 2 (Reputable)**

In February 2026, the Supreme Court limited the use of the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) for broad-based tariff actions. The administration pivoted to Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 — a temporary balance-of-payments emergency authority — imposing a 10% global tariff while shifting to Section 301 investigations for more durable legal footing.

**Why it matters:** This is a structural shift in how executive tariff power works. The legal basis for broad trade actions is now fragmented across multiple authorities, creating ongoing litigation risk and policy uncertainty.
