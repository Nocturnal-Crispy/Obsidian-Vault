## 3. Project Glasswing Update: Claude Mythos Found 23,019 Bugs in Open Source
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1 (Anthropic, Help Net Security, The Hacker News, Cloudflare)

Anthropic's Project Glasswing — giving partners access to Claude Mythos for vulnerability scanning — has now identified 23,019 issues across 1,000+ open-source projects, with 6,202 rated high or critical severity. Partners include AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, Linux Foundation, Microsoft, Nvidia, and Palo Alto Networks.

Notable finding: Mythos discovered a certificate-forging vulnerability in wolfSSL (used by billions of devices) and constructed a working exploit. Anthropic plans to expand Glasswing with US and allied governments but will restrict general Mythos-class access until stronger safeguards are developed.

**Strategic Impact:** This validates the "AI for defense" thesis — frontier models can find vulnerabilities faster than human security researchers. The wolfSSL exploit demonstrates real-world offensive capability. The restricted access model signals Anthropic treating this as dual-use technology. This is the strongest evidence yet that AI is reshaping cybersecurity economics.
