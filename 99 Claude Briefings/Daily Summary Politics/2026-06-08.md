## 1. Iran Ceasefire — 60-Day MOU Framework Emerging; Hormuz Terms Outlined
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1 (The Hill, CNBC, CNN, Al Jazeera, ABC News)

The Iran ceasefire situation has evolved significantly since June 4:

- **MOU framework:** U.S. and Iran reportedly reached a preliminary memorandum of understanding to extend the ceasefire for 60 days and begin permanent peace negotiations
- **Hormuz terms:** Shipping through the Strait to be "unrestricted" — no tolls, no harassment. Iran has 30 days to remove all mines
- **U.S. blockade:** Lifted "in proportion to restoration of commercial shipping" through the Strait
- **Sanctions:** U.S. to waive certain sanctions, allowing Iran to sell oil freely
- **Awaiting Trump approval:** Framework still needs presidential sign-off
- **Ongoing tensions:** Iranian military warned that if hostilities continue, response "will be more severe"; Israel-Iran strikes continue
- **Trump position:** "Final negotiations on 'Peace' are proceeding"; blockade remains until deal is reached

**Strategic Impact:** The 60-day MOU represents the most concrete diplomatic framework since the conflict began. The Hormuz terms — unrestricted shipping, 30-day mine clearance, proportional blockade lifting — are detailed enough to suggest real negotiation has occurred. However, the gap between framework and execution remains wide: Israel-Iran strikes continue, Trump hasn't approved it, and Iran's warning about escalation creates ambiguity. If this MOU is signed, the immediate impact is on global energy and fertilizer shipping costs. Agriculture and manufacturing sectors watching closely.

## 2. House Passes Ukraine Support Act 226-195 — Via Discharge Petition
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (Washington Post, CBS News, NOTUS, Newsweek)

The House passed Ukraine aid legislation on June 5 in a dramatic procedural maneuver:

- **Vote:** 226-195; 18 Republicans broke with party leadership and Trump
- **Mechanism:** Discharge petition (filed July 2025, reached 218 signatures in May) forced floor vote over GOP leadership objections
- **Content:** $1B+ in security and reconstruction funding; up to $8B in military financing loans; new Russia sanctions targeting key industries
- **Lone Democratic dissent:** Rep. Ilhan Omar (D-MN) voted against
- **Senate outlook:** Faces "long odds" + potential presidential veto
- **Significance:** First successful discharge petition on a major foreign policy bill in modern congressional history

**Strategic Impact:** The procedural story is as significant as the policy: 18 Republicans defied both their leadership and the President to force Ukraine aid through. This is the strongest evidence yet of institutional GOP fracture on foreign policy. Combined with the Iowa primary loss (June 3), Trump's grip on the party shows cracks on multiple fronts. The Senate pathway is difficult and a veto likely, but the vote count (226) matters as a signal of bipartisan floor strength on Ukraine. The Russia sanctions component adds economic pressure leverage.
