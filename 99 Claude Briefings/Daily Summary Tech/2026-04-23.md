# Tech Intelligence — Thursday, April 23, 2026

## 1. Google Cloud Next: Ironwood TPU GA + TPU 8 Dual-Track Architecture Unveiled
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Google Blog, Google Cloud, CNBC, The Register, SemiAnalysis)**

Google made its seventh-generation TPU, Ironwood, generally available at Cloud Next in Las Vegas. Ironwood delivers 4.6 petaFLOPS per chip and 42.5 exaFLOPS in a 9,216-chip superpod — more powerful than the world's largest supercomputer. It offers 10x peak performance over TPU v5p and 4x better performance per chip vs. TPU v6e (Trillium). More significantly, Google previewed its eighth-generation architecture with a historic split: **TPU 8t "Sunfish"** (Broadcom-designed training chip) and **TPU 8i "Zebrafish"** (MediaTek-designed inference chip), both targeting TSMC 2nm and late 2027. Sunfish scales to 9,600 TPUs with 2PB shared memory, achieving 3x Ironwood's processing power at 2x performance/watt. Zebrafish delivers 80% better performance per dollar for inference with 288GB HBM3e and 384MB on-chip SRAM. This is the first time Google has purpose-built separate training and inference TPUs — a direct strategic response to the inference cost war with Nvidia. Anthropic's 3.5GW deal makes it the anchor customer for both generations.

## 2. Anthropic Expands to Europe — First International Data Center Team
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (CNBC), Tier 2 (Data Center Dynamics, The Decoder)**

One day after announcing its $30B run rate, Anthropic posted a "Transaction Principal" role in London at £225K–£270K ($304K–$364K) to negotiate European data center capacity deals. This is the first time Anthropic is building a dedicated data center team outside the US. The role targets the FLAP-D hubs (Frankfurt, London, Amsterdam, Paris, Dublin) plus Nordics and Southern Europe. A similar role was posted in Australia. The strategic read: Anthropic is shifting from a US-compute-only company to a global infrastructure player, mirroring the path Google and Microsoft took 15 years ago. The timing aligns with EU data sovereignty requirements and the need to serve European enterprise clients with local inference capacity.

## 4. OpenAI Launches ChatGPT for Clinicians + GPT-5.4 Medical Benchmark
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (OpenAI Blog), Tier 2 (Neowin, Fierce Healthcare, The Decoder)**

OpenAI released ChatGPT for Clinicians — a free clinical AI workspace for verified US physicians, NPs, PAs, and pharmacists. GPT-5.4 running in the Clinicians workspace scored 59.0 on HealthBench Professional vs. 43.7 for human doctors (who had unlimited time and internet access). Physician advisors tested 6,924 conversations and rated 99.6% of responses as safe and accurate. This is OpenAI's most aggressive vertical play yet — giving away a premium product to establish clinical AI as a platform. The competitive implications for Anthropic's healthcare partnerships and Google's Med-PaLM are significant. International expansion will follow via the Better Evidence Network pilot.
