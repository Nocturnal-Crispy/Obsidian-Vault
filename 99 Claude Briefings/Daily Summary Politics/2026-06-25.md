#### 1099 reporting thresholds reset for 2026 under OBBBA — relevant to your consulting income
`Heat: Medium` · `Momentum: SUSTAINED` · `Tier 1 — Confirmed` · `Category: Small-Biz/1099`

- **What:** Under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, the 1099-NEC / 1099-MISC reporting threshold rose from $600 to $2,000 effective for payments on/after Jan 1, 2026. Form 1099-K reverted to the old $20,000-and-200-transactions trigger.
- **Why it matters (for you):** As a 1099 consultant you'll both receive and (if you pay subcontractors) issue fewer forms. Clients paying you under $2,000/yr may no longer issue a 1099-NEC — but the income is still reportable. Keep your own records; don't rely on forms to reconcile.
- **Source:** Calibre CPA / OnPay — https://calibrecpa.com/tax-regulation-reporting/2026-reporting-changes-for-1099-forms/

#### IRS leans on AI amid ~25% workforce reduction; no new federal AI law
`Heat: Low` · `Momentum: SUSTAINED` · `Tier 2 — Reputable` · `Category: AI Regulation`

- **What:** The IRS has cut ~25% of its workforce over the past year and is deploying AI for audits, fraud detection and taxpayer services. There is still no comprehensive federal AI statute; state-level rules continue to vary.
- **Why it matters (for you):** AI-driven audit selection raises the bar on clean documentation for Schedule C / self-employment filings. The patchwork of state AI rules matters if you ship AI features to clients across state lines.
- **Source:** US Chamber CO / IRS — https://www.uschamber.com/co/events/small-business-update/small-business-update-2026-economic-outlook-tax-changes-and-ai-trends
