## Technology

**Anthropic becomes most valuable AI startup.** The company's $30B+ round at $900B eclipsed OpenAI's $852B. Revenue trajectory is staggering: $50B ARR projected by end of June, up from ~$19B earlier in Q2. The Milan office opening signals European enterprise push.

**Project Glasswing delivered real results.** Claude Mythos scanned 1,000+ open-source projects and found 23,019 bugs (6,202 high/critical severity). The wolfSSL certificate-forging exploit demonstrated genuine offensive capability. This changes the economics of vulnerability research.

**Microsoft Build 2026 looms.** Project Polaris (homegrown coding model for Copilot), Copilot Workspace (agentic PR generation), and Windows-as-agent-platform represent Microsoft's biggest push toward reducing OpenAI dependency.

**Nvidia pushing boundaries.** XFRA residential AI compute nodes with PulteGroup. Vera Rubin shipping H2 2026. Jensen Huang declared 2026 "the year of inference" with $1T+ cumulative demand projection.

**Memory shortage hits consumers.** Steam Deck OLED prices jumped up to 50% ($649→$949 for 1TB). Nvidia skipping gaming GPUs in 2026 entirely. AI infrastructure is directly crowding consumer silicon.

## Agriculture

### Key Developments

**Worst spring drought on record.** 62%+ of the Lower 48 in drought. DSCI hit 202 — highest in Drought Monitor history. This is now a food security story.

**Winter wheat catastrophe.** USDA forecasts lowest acreage since 1919. 32% national abandonment rate. Nebraska 82% poor-to-very-poor. Kansas 58%. Oklahoma 48%. 70% of crop in drought areas.

**Cattle herd rebuild stalling.** 79% of beef cow herd in drought-affected areas. Ranchers approaching forced liquidation decisions by July if pasture conditions don't improve. WASDE projects lower beef production through 2027.

**HPAI remains elevated.** 18+ states affected (poultry + dairy cattle). Arctic mammalian detections continue (polar bears, walruses). CDC maintains low human risk.

**Grain markets conflicted.** Corn near 6-week lows ($4.57/bu) despite drought risk — trade uncertainty caps upside. Wheat declining for 5 consecutive sessions despite acreage crisis. Markets appear to be underpricing drought severity.
