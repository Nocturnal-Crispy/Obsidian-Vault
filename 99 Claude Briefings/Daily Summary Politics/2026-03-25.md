## Story 2: DHS Shutdown Day 40 — GOP Proposal Sent, Democrats Counter, Deal Uncertain
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

The DHS shutdown enters its 40th day with a formal proposal now on the table but both parties showing buyer's remorse.

**The GOP offer:** Senate Majority Leader Thune formally sent a proposal Tuesday morning that funds 94% of DHS ($5.5B withheld for ICE Enforcement and Removal Operations). White House border czar Tom Homan met with a bipartisan group of senators.

**Democratic response:** Senate Democrats are sending a counteroffer that includes "significant reform" provisions for ICE. Minority Leader Schumer insists Democrats won't agree to funding without agency reforms — a harder line than the previous week's discussions suggested.

**The Trump factor:** Washington Post reports Trump "isn't sold" on the GOP proposal, which creates a three-way negotiation problem: Republicans, Democrats, and the White House may all have different bottom lines.

**TSA crisis deepening:** Hundreds of TSA agents have quit in recent weeks. TSA agents will miss another paycheck Friday. Spring break travel is compounding the operational strain. Union leaders are calling it a "national disgrace."

**Why it matters:** Yesterday's optimism about a deal this week has cooled. The three-way dynamics (GOP caucus, Democrats, Trump) make closure harder than a simple two-party negotiation. The TSA crisis is becoming a concrete public safety issue — if agents continue quitting and airports face reduced screening, the political pressure will shift dramatically.
