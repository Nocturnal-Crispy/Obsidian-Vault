## Livestock

### 1. Record Drought Threatens US Cow Herd Rebuild — 79% of Beef Cattle in Drought Areas
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, Drought.gov, DTN Progressive Farmer)

The drought situation for livestock has escalated significantly:

- 79% of the beef cow herd in the 26 states accounting for 88% of 2026 production is currently in drought-affected areas
- 61% of all US cattle are in drought zones
- The Drought Severity and Coverage Index (DSCI) hit 202 — the highest end-of-April value in Drought Monitor history (since 2000)
- Ranchers are in "waiting game" mode — forced liquidation decisions approaching if pasture conditions don't improve by July

USDA WASDE-671 continues projecting lower beef production through 2027 as herd rebuilding is now effectively stalled or reversing in drought regions.

**Strategic Impact:** The herd rebuild that began in late 2024 is being undone by drought. Forced liquidation would create short-term supply surge (lower prices) followed by structural shortage (higher prices 2027-2028). This is the most significant long-term US beef supply risk in a decade.

### 2. HPAI Surveillance Posture Remains Elevated
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (APHIS, CDC)

H5N1 HPAI continues across US poultry and dairy cattle (18+ states). No new escalation events since Friday. CDC maintains "low" public health risk. Arctic mammalian detections (polar bears, walruses, arctic foxes) continue to signal viral adaptation but no new human clusters.

**Strategic Impact:** Biosecurity remains critical. Status quo — elevated but not escalating.

## Crops

### 3. Winter Wheat Crisis Deepens: Lowest Acreage Since 1919, 32% Abandonment
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, Time, ABC News, UCS)

The winter wheat situation has worsened beyond Friday's report:

- USDA forecasts 2026 wheat acreage as the **lowest since 1919**
- Projected 32% abandonment rate nationally
- Nebraska: 82% of winter wheat rated very poor to poor
- Kansas: 93% headed, 58% poor to very poor
- Oklahoma: 48% poor to very poor
- 70% of the winter wheat crop is in drought-affected areas
- 98% of cotton crop also in drought zones

Drought now covers 62%+ of the Lower 48 — described as the **worst spring drought on record**. The DSCI has remained above 200 continuously since late April.

**Strategic Impact:** This is approaching food security significance. Wheat production will be dramatically below trend. Combined with global supply tightness, US wheat exports will decline, supporting higher bread/food prices domestically. Corn and soybean yield risk rises if drought persists through July pollination.

### 4. Corn and Soybean Planting Ahead of Average but Yield Risk Mounting
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA NASS, RFD-TV)

Corn and soybean planting progress remains ahead of the five-year average. However, the expanding drought is creating a growing gap between planted acreage and probable harvested acreage. The critical window is late June-July: if drought persists through corn pollination, yield losses could be catastrophic.

**Strategic Impact:** Markets are not yet pricing in severe corn yield failure. A hot, dry June would trigger rapid futures repricing.

## Gardening

### 5. Zone 6a/6b (Hermitage, PA): End of May Maintenance Window
**Heat:** Low | **Momentum:** SEASONAL | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Penn State Extension)

End of May guidance for zone 6:

- **Now:** All warm-season crops should be in ground. Succession sow beans, lettuce, radish every 2 weeks.
- **Maintenance:** Deep water (1-1.5"/week minimum) given drought conditions. Mulch all beds 2-3" to conserve moisture. Side-dress tomatoes and corn with nitrogen.
- **Pest watch:**
  - Spotted lanternfly nymphs increasingly active — check tree of heaven, grapes, hops
  - Squash vine borer adults emerged — apply Bt or row cover now before egg-laying
  - Japanese beetles approaching — handpick or neem oil spray at first sign
  - Monitor for tomato hornworm as plants size up
- **Harvest:** Strawberries, lettuce, radish, peas, early greens
- **Planning:** Start fall brassica seeds indoors (late June transplant target)

**Strategic Impact:** Drought conditions in the broader region demand extra irrigation attention for home gardens. Municipal water restrictions possible if drought persists — consider rain barrel installation.
