## Story 1: Iran War — Congress Demands Exit Plan, DHS Shutdown Enters Week 5
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

The US-Iran conflict, now in its fourth week, faces mounting congressional pressure for an exit strategy. At least 13 US military personnel have died and over 230 wounded. A $200 billion Pentagon war funding request remains pending at the White House. The Senate failed again Saturday to advance a DHS funding bill — the procedural vote failed 47-37 with 16 senators absent. The department has now been shut down for over a month.

Key escalation: Trump called NATO allies "COWARDS" and a "PAPER TIGER" after they refused to send forces to help reopen the Strait of Hormuz. No allied nation has committed military assets to the strait operation.

The Strait of Hormuz crisis remains severe: tanker traffic dropped ~70%, Iran has conducted 21 confirmed attacks on merchant ships, Brent crude peaked at $126/barrel, and over 150 ships remain anchored outside the strait.

**Why it matters:** The convergence of a hot war, a cabinet-level government shutdown, spiking oil prices, and allied non-cooperation represents the most complex US crisis environment since 2001. The DHS shutdown during an active military conflict is historically unprecedented.

## Story 2: Natanz Strike Aftermath — Second Attack on Nuclear Infrastructure
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

Following yesterday's second US-Israeli strike on Iran's Natanz nuclear facility, the diplomatic and military situation continues to evolve. Iran's atomic energy organization confirmed no radioactive leakage. Israeli Defense Minister Katz warned strikes would intensify this coming week. Iran has retaliated with ballistic missiles targeting Diego Garcia, expanding the conflict beyond the Middle East theater.

The UN atomic energy chief stated that war cannot entirely eliminate Iran's nuclear program — a significant admission that the stated military objective may be unachievable.

**Why it matters:** The second strike on Natanz crosses a significant escalation threshold. If the UN assessment is correct that military strikes cannot eliminate Iran's nuclear capability, the fundamental premise of the campaign is being challenged by the international nuclear authority itself.
