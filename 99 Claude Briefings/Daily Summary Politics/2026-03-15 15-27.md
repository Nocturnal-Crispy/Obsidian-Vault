## Policy & Regulation

### Anthropic vs. Pentagon — AI Safety Guardrails Become a National Security Issue
Anthropic filed suit on March 9 against the Trump administration after being designated a "supply chain risk" by the Pentagon — a designation normally reserved for entities linked to foreign adversaries. The core dispute: Anthropic insisted its AI models not be used for autonomous weapons or mass surveillance of U.S. citizens. When contract negotiations broke down over these red lines, the Pentagon blacklisted the company. The designation requires defense contractors to certify they do not use Claude. Anthropic argues the action violates constitutional free speech and due process protections. The case could jeopardize hundreds of millions in revenue.

**What changed:** The federal government has escalated from policy disagreement to punitive legal designation against a major AI company over safety restrictions.
**Who is affected:** Anthropic, defense contractors, the entire AI industry's relationship with military procurement, AI safety research community.
**What to watch:** Court rulings on the supply chain risk designation; whether Congress intervenes; whether OpenAI or Google face similar pressure; precedent for AI companies maintaining ethical red lines.
**Trend:** New Trend

Georgetown University's Center for Security and Emerging Technology is providing analysis on the role of AI in the conflict.

**Who is affected:** Congressional oversight committees, AI companies with military contracts, international humanitarian law frameworks, civilian populations in conflict zones.

### U.S. Drafts Global AI Chip Export Controls — Beyond China
The Commerce Department has drafted regulations that would require U.S. government permits for AI chip shipments to any country in the world — not just China. This would give Washington unprecedented control over which nations can build AI training infrastructure. The move represents a dramatic expansion of export control scope.

**What changed:** Export controls expanding from targeted adversary restrictions to global gatekeeping of AI hardware.
**Who is affected:** Allied nations (EU, Japan, South Korea, India) building sovereign AI capabilities; NVIDIA and AMD revenue; global AI development timelines.
**What to watch:** Allied government reactions; whether this drives accelerated domestic chip development programs globally; final rule publication timeline.
**Trend:** Escalating Trend

### Trump Approved NVIDIA H20 Sales to China for 15% Revenue Cut
In a policy contradiction, the Trump administration approved licenses for NVIDIA to sell H20 and H200 chips to China in exchange for a 15% cut of revenue from those sales — reversing earlier Biden-era restrictions. This has frustrated GOP China hawks who see it as undermining the export control regime.

**What changed:** The administration is simultaneously expanding global export controls while creating carve-outs for China sales with a revenue-sharing arrangement.
**Who is affected:** NVIDIA, China's AI development trajectory, bipartisan export control consensus, allied countries subject to stricter controls than China.
**What to watch:** Congressional reaction; whether the revenue-sharing model is formalized or challenged; impact on allied trust in U.S. export control consistency.
**Trend:** New Trend

### Federal vs. State AI Regulation — Executive Order Task Force Active
Escalation: Yes — The Trump administration's executive order creating a task force to challenge state AI laws in court is now operational. The Senate previously rejected a 10-year moratorium on state AI laws by a 99-1 vote. Colorado's Consumer Protections for AI (requiring "reasonable care" against algorithmic discrimination) is delayed to June 30. Illinois amended its Human Rights Act to prohibit AI-based discriminatory hiring. The patchwork continues to grow as federal preemption fails legislatively, forcing the administration to pursue it through executive action and litigation.

**What changed:** The executive task force is now actively litigating — moving from policy proposal to enforcement action against state AI regulations.
**Who is affected:** AI companies navigating compliance across states, state legislators, consumers protected by state-level AI laws.
**What to watch:** First court decisions on federal challenges to state AI laws; whether Colorado's June 30 implementation proceeds; midterm election impact on state-level AI legislation.
**Trend:** Escalating Trend
