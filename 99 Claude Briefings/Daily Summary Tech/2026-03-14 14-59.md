## Security

### Microsoft March 2026 Patch Tuesday — 79 Vulnerabilities, Two Zero-Days
Microsoft patched 79 vulnerabilities including two publicly disclosed zero-days. Critical highlights include CVE-2026-23669, a print queue RCE reminiscent of PrintNightmare; CVE-2026-26144, a critical XSS in Excel enabling Copilot Agent data exfiltration; and CVE-2026-26110/26113, RCE flaws exploitable via the preview pane without opening files. Six vulnerabilities are rated "more likely" to be exploited — all privilege escalation vectors.

**What changed:** Patch available now. The Copilot Agent exfiltration vector (CVE-2026-26144) is a novel attack surface.
**Who is affected:** All Windows/Office enterprise environments. The preview-pane RCEs are particularly dangerous for email-heavy organizations.
**What to watch:** Exploit activity in the wild, especially for the privilege escalation cluster.
**Trend:** Continuing Trend (monthly patch cycle, but Copilot-targeting vulnerability is novel)

### Meta Drops Instagram End-to-End Encryption — Effective May 8
Meta announced it will remove E2EE from Instagram DMs starting May 8, 2026, citing low opt-in rates and directing users to WhatsApp for encrypted messaging. Industry analysts link the reversal to increasing law enforcement pressure globally. Message contents will become accessible for platform moderation and regulatory compliance.

**What changed:** Major privacy policy reversal from a platform with 2B+ users.
**Who is affected:** Instagram users who relied on encrypted DMs; privacy advocates; law enforcement.
**What to watch:** Whether this triggers regulatory action in the EU under GDPR; user migration to Signal/WhatsApp.
**Trend:** New Trend
