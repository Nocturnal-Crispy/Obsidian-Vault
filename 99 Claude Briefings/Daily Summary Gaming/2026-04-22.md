# Gaming Intelligence — Wednesday, April 22, 2026

## 1. Xbox Game Pass Price Drop + Call of Duty Removed from Day-One — Industry Restructuring
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Xbox Wire, Bloomberg, CNBC, Variety, Windows Central, GameSpot)**

Microsoft made the most consequential Game Pass change since launch: Ultimate drops from $29.99 to $22.99/month, PC Game Pass from $16.49 to $13.99/month. The trade-off — future Call of Duty titles will no longer join Game Pass at launch, instead arriving approximately one year later during the following holiday season. Existing COD titles remain in the library. This is a structural pivot. Microsoft is effectively admitting that day-one AAA inclusion at scale is economically unsustainable — COD's inclusion was estimated to cost $300-500M annually in foregone sales. The price cut is designed to retain subscribers through the transition. New Xbox CEO (post-Spencer era) framed it as "recommitting to gamers." The community reaction is split: price-sensitive subscribers welcome the cut; COD-focused subscribers feel betrayed. The strategic read: Microsoft is moving Game Pass from "everything day one" toward "deep catalog + selective day-one," a more sustainable model that still undercuts PlayStation Plus on value. This is the biggest subscription gaming story of 2026.

## 2. Diablo IV: Lord of Hatred — 6 Days Out, Blizzard's Biggest Expansion Yet
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Blizzard, Battle.net, Steam), Tier 2 (PC Gamer, Icy Veins)**

Diablo IV's second expansion launches April 28 on all platforms. Lord of Hatred is substantially larger than Vessel of Hatred: two new classes (Paladin and Warlock), a new region (Skovos), and overhauled endgame systems. Pre-purchasing any edition grants instant access to the Paladin class and Vessel of Hatred expansion. PC Gamer's early review is positive. This is Blizzard's shot at recapturing the ARPG audience that has drifted to Path of Exile 2 since it went premium. The timing is deliberate — landing one week after the Game Pass price restructuring, Diablo IV benefits from renewed attention on the gaming subscription ecosystem. The two-class addition is the largest single-expansion class injection in Diablo franchise history.

## 3. Steam Trends — R.E.P.O. and Schedule I Dominate Indie Charts
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW (vs last briefing) | Source: Tier 1 (Steam, SteamDB), Tier 3 (community)**

Both R.E.P.O. and Schedule I peaked at ~459K concurrent players in April 2026, making them the breakout indie hits of the month. These are extraordinary numbers — comparable to AAA launch peaks. The broader Steam ecosystem is trending toward 45M concurrent users by late 2026. Meanwhile, the perennial top 5 remains structurally stable: Counter-Strike 2, Dota 2, PUBG, and ARC Raiders hold the daily peaks. Path of Exile 2 continues to outperform Diablo IV and Last Epoch in concurrent counts since going premium.

## 4. 2XKO Adds Akali + Local Duos Feature
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Riot Games)**

Riot Games added the League of Legends champion Akali to 2XKO alongside a new Local Duos feature for couch co-op play. 2XKO (formerly Project L) continues its steady character rollout ahead of what is expected to be a 2026 full launch. The Local Duos addition signals Riot is targeting the casual/social fighting game audience, not just the competitive FGC core.

## 5. EVE Online — Capsuleer Day XXIII: Warpath Incoming
**Heat: Low | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (CCP Games)**

CCP Games announced Capsuleer Day XXIII with the "Warpath" update incoming. EVE Online continues its 23-year run with regular content cadence. Community engagement remains stable via Patreon creators (Lorumerth content active in inbox). Niche but durable — EVE's longevity is itself a signal about persistent-world game design.
