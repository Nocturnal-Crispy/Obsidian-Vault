## Story 1: Pentagon Prepares Ground Operations in Iran — Kharg Island Seizure Under Consideration
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source Tier:** 2 (Reputable — Washington Post, Al Jazeera)
**Categories:** Security, Market Signals (cross-linked to [[99 Claude Briefings/Daily Summary Politics/2026-03-29]])

The Washington Post reports the Pentagon is preparing for weeks of limited ground operations in Iran, including potential seizure of Kharg Island — Iran's primary oil export hub — and raids on coastal sites near the Strait of Hormuz. Special operations and conventional infantry troops would be involved. White House press secretary Karoline Leavitt stated the president has not yet made a decision, emphasizing the Pentagon is providing "maximum optionality."

Over a dozen US soldiers were injured in a Houthi attack on a Saudi base, marking the first significant US casualties from the expanded conflict. Iran's internet blackout has now lasted 30 days (696+ hours).

**Why it matters:** The potential seizure of Kharg Island would be the most consequential military escalation since the war began. Kharg handles roughly 90% of Iran's oil exports. Seizing it would cripple Iran's remaining revenue stream but would also create a hostage situation for global oil markets — any damage to Kharg infrastructure during a contested seizure could spike oil prices dramatically. The shift from air strikes to ground operations crosses a threshold that historically extends conflicts by orders of magnitude in duration and cost. For tech: energy price spikes directly increase data center operating costs and could delay AI infrastructure buildouts.

## Story 3: AMD-Meta $60B AI Data Center Deal — Largest Chip Deal in History
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source Tier:** 2 (Reputable — Investing.com)
**Categories:** GPU / Semiconductor, AI

Meta awarded AMD a landmark ~$60 billion, five-year agreement to power its next-generation 6-gigawatt AI data centers using custom AMD Instinct MI450 GPUs and 6th Gen EPYC "Venice" CPUs. AMD stock surged 8% on the announcement. Deployment begins H2 2026. AMD is now up 88% over the past year versus Nvidia's 46%.

AMD also expanded through a multi-billion dollar AI partnership with Seoul and record-breaking 34-qubit quantum simulations with Samsung on HBM4 memory.

**Why it matters:** This is the clearest evidence yet that Nvidia's data center GPU monopoly is fracturing. Meta choosing AMD for a 6GW buildout — one of the largest AI infrastructure projects ever — signals that AMD's Instinct line has reached competitive parity for key workloads. The 88% vs 46% stock performance gap reflects the market repricing AMD's AI trajectory. Nvidia still holds 92% market share, but the direction of travel is unmistakable.

## Story 4: Google TurboQuant and Vera Rubin — Inference Economics Transformation Continues
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)
**Categories:** AI, GPU / Semiconductor

Google's TurboQuant 6x memory compression continues to reshape LLM inference economics. Memory chip stocks (Micron, WDC, SanDisk) remain under pressure but Morgan Stanley's Jevons Paradox thesis is gaining traction — cheaper inference means more inference. Meanwhile, Nvidia's Vera Rubin platform (full production, 3nm TSMC) delivers 3.5x training and 5x inference improvements over Blackwell.

Google also released Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, delivering 2.5x faster response times at $0.25 per million input tokens — further driving down the cost floor for AI services.

**Why it matters:** The convergence of TurboQuant, Vera Rubin, and aggressive model pricing (Flash-Lite at $0.25/M tokens) is compressing AI inference costs at a rate that could enable entirely new application categories. Enterprise AI adoption barriers are falling rapidly.
