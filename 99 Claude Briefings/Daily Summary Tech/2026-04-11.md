# Tech Intelligence — Saturday, April 11, 2026

## 1. Iran-Linked Hackers Targeting US Critical Infrastructure — PLC Exposure Surface
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 (Cybersecurity Dive, BleepingComputer; cross-ref Tier 3 CISA advisory)**

As the Islamabad talks open, Iranian-linked threat actors are actively targeting US critical-infrastructure networks with a focus on thousands of internet-exposed Rockwell Automation programmable logic controllers. The campaign is broad, opportunistic, and aligned with the ceasefire-leverage window — the pattern strongly resembles the 2020-era IRGC "CyberAv3ngers" targeting of Unitronics PLCs, but the target count is an order of magnitude larger. The timing is deliberate: CISA is operating at degraded capacity through Day 57 of the DHS shutdown, Anthropic's Project Glasswing defender program is still stabilizing its rollout, and the physical ceasefire is visibly fragile. Water utilities, power substations, and industrial control systems remain the highest-risk categories. Expect emergency advisories in the next 24-48 hours. This is the single clearest cyber escalation tied to the Iran war, and it is happening now.

## 2. Nvidia GPU Shortage Hits Nvidia's Own Research Teams
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Fortune, Nvidia VP quote)**

Bryan Catanzaro, Nvidia's VP of Applied Deep Learning Research, publicly confirmed that even Nvidia's internal research teams cannot get enough GPUs. The statement is remarkable for three reasons. First, it validates the signal from the CoreWeave-Meta $21B expansion and the Vera Rubin backlog: demand still vastly exceeds supply at every tier of the allocation stack. Second, it explains why Anthropic, OpenAI and Meta are all moving on custom silicon simultaneously — the bottleneck is no longer cost, it is physical availability. Third, it is the cleanest signal yet that the 2026 AI infrastructure shortage is structural, not cyclical; every hyperscaler deal announced this quarter is effectively pre-consuming 2027-2029 supply. The practical read: the custom-chip roadmaps being pursued by frontier labs are no longer optional hedges, they are load-bearing.

## 3. OpenAI Pauses UK Stargate Data Center — IPO Posture Shifts
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 (Multiple outlets; UK press)**

OpenAI has paused its planned Stargate UK data-center build as it tightens spending ahead of a potential late-2026 public listing and narrows operational focus back onto the core ChatGPT business. The pause is notable in two directions. On one axis it is a retreat — a walkback from the "build everywhere, infinite compute" framing that defined the Altman thesis through 2025. On the other axis it is a pre-IPO discipline signal to the market: OpenAI is preparing to show investors a capex story that looks more like Meta-2022 than DeepMind-2019. Meanwhile, OpenAI is publicly claiming a compute advantage over Anthropic, citing ~$600B cumulative infra spend projected through 2030 against Anthropic's roughly $50B committed. Anthropic's counter is that it is growing revenue roughly 3x faster — and is narrowing the overall gap as a result. The two labs are now running two fundamentally different capital strategies into the same market.

## 4. WordPress Smart Slider 3 Pro Supply-Chain Attack — 6-Hour Poisoned Window
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 (BleepingComputer, cybersecurity advisories)**

Unknown threat actors hijacked the update mechanism for the Smart Slider 3 Pro plugin (used across WordPress and Joomla) on April 7, distributing a backdoored build for approximately six hours before detection. Any site that auto-updated inside that window should be treated as potentially compromised. This is the second major CMS-plugin supply-chain incident in 2026 and fits the broader pattern — LiteLLM (March), now Smart Slider 3 Pro — of attackers targeting the update layer rather than the software itself. The blast radius is smaller than LiteLLM but the TTPs are converging: compromise the distribution pipeline, not the code.

## 5. Citizen Lab — Ad-Data Surveillance Targeting 500M Devices
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 (Citizen Lab, reported April 11)**

Citizen Lab reports that law-enforcement agencies, including Hungarian domestic intelligence and US departments, have been leveraging advertising-based data pipelines to track approximately 500 million devices. The mechanism: buying or ingesting mobile-ad exchange bid-stream data to derive location, device-ID and behavioral graphs, then cross-referencing against persons of interest. The scale, the named agencies, and the timing — 24 hours before the Hungarian election — are all noteworthy. For US companies this tightens the already-hot debate about data brokers and state-adjacent surveillance pipelines; for the EU it will feed directly into the next round of AI Act and GDPR enforcement conversations.
