# Tech Intelligence — Wednesday, April 22, 2026

## 1. Anthropic Passes $30B Run Rate — Locks In 3.5GW Google-Broadcom Compute Deal
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Anthropic blog, Bloomberg, CNBC, TechCrunch)**

Anthropic's annualized revenue has surpassed $30 billion — more than tripling from ~$9B at end of 2025. The company now counts over 1,000 enterprise clients spending $1M+ annually, double the count from two months ago. Simultaneously, Anthropic expanded its partnership with Google and Broadcom to secure 3.5 gigawatts of TPU compute capacity from 2027, up from 1GW in 2026. Broadcom CEO Hock Tan confirmed the arrangement; Mizuho estimates Broadcom will earn $21B in AI revenue from Anthropic in 2026 and $42B in 2027. This is a structural bet: Anthropic is locking in compute at a scale that rivals Meta and Microsoft's GPU commitments, but on Google TPUs rather than Nvidia GPUs. Combined with MCP's 97M+ installs making it the de facto agent protocol, Anthropic is now competing on three fronts simultaneously — model quality (Opus 4.6/Sonnet 4.6), protocol dominance (MCP), and infrastructure scale (TPU compute). The $30B run rate also narrows the revenue gap with OpenAI ($25B+), making this a genuine two-horse commercial race.

## 2. Morgan Stanley: Agentic AI Shifts $60B in Value from GPUs to CPUs and Memory
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 2 (Morgan Stanley research note via Yahoo Finance, ANI, multiple financial outlets)**

Morgan Stanley published a research note arguing that the transition from single-task AI to autonomous agentic systems is structurally shifting economic value from GPUs to CPUs and memory. Key findings: CPU-side orchestration accounts for 50-90% of total workload latency in agentic systems; agentic AI could add $32.5-60B to the data-center CPU TAM by 2030; agentic workloads could drive 15-45 exabytes of additional DRAM demand by 2030, equivalent to 26-77% of 2027 annual DRAM supply. Morgan Stanley named Micron and SanDisk as preferred plays and expects tight supply through at least 2027. This is a meaningful structural thesis — if correct, it implies Nvidia's GPU monopoly on AI value capture has a ceiling, and the next wave of AI infrastructure spending benefits a broader set of chipmakers. Intel and AMD's CPU businesses would be direct beneficiaries.

## 3. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Unite Against Chinese Model Distillation
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Bloomberg), Tier 2 (Seeking Alpha, Japan Times)**

The three leading US AI labs are now actively collaborating through the Frontier Model Forum to detect and counter adversarial distillation — the practice of Chinese competitors extracting capabilities from frontier US models to train their own. The firms are sharing detection signals and enforcement patterns. This is the first coordinated defensive action by the three rivals and signals that model IP protection has moved from a theoretical concern to an operational priority. The backdrop is significant: with Anthropic at $30B, OpenAI at $25B+, and Google's Gemini ecosystem growing rapidly, the commercial stakes of model theft are now measured in tens of billions of dollars. The collaboration also implicitly signals that technical countermeasures alone are insufficient — this requires industry coordination.

## 4. Nvidia-Intel Collaboration + SiFive Hits $3.65B Valuation
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Nvidia Newsroom, TechCrunch), Tier 2 (CNBC)**

Two developments compound the GPU-to-broader-silicon shift. First, Nvidia and Intel announced a collaboration to build x86 SoCs integrating Nvidia RTX GPU chiplets for AI PCs, with Nvidia investing $5B in Intel stock at $23.28/share. This is notable: Nvidia is simultaneously competing with Intel on data center GPUs while partnering on PC silicon, a sign that the AI chip market is too large for zero-sum thinking. Second, Nvidia-backed SiFive raised $400M at a $3.65B valuation for open RISC-V AI chip designs. Nvidia is hedging — backing alternative CPU architectures (RISC-V) while partnering with the incumbent (Intel x86), all while maintaining GPU dominance. The $5B Intel investment also provides Nvidia a strategic stake in Intel's foundry ambitions, potentially securing future packaging capacity.

## 5. CISA Adds 14 KEV Entries — Microsoft Patches 167 Vulns Including SharePoint Zero-Day
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (CISA, Microsoft), Tier 2 (SecurityWeek, Hacker News)**

CISA added six new flaws to KEV (including Fortinet FortiClient EMS SQL injection, CVE-2026-21643, CVSS 9.1) plus eight more including three Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager flaws, with federal deadlines through May 2026. Separately, Microsoft's April Patch Tuesday addressed 167 vulnerabilities including two zero-days, one actively exploited in SharePoint Server. A new RDP warning mechanism was also introduced to combat phishing via weaponized .rdp files. The volume remains high but manageable — the concern is that CISA's operational capacity remains degraded under the DHS shutdown (now Day 67), reducing the agency's ability to coordinate response at scale.
