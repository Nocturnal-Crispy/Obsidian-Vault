## Livestock

### 1. Drought-Cattle Crisis — Liquidation Window Now ~2 Weeks; Cash Cattle $253-258
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, Drought.gov, Xinhua/Reuters, CME, Barchart)

The drought-cattle crisis continues with the decision window shrinking:

- **Drought:** 58.4% of lower 48 states in drought (down slightly from 60%+ in early June — marginal improvement)
- **Cattle in drought:** 57% of U.S. cattle inventory in moderate drought or worse (down from 79% of beef cow herd — different measurement basis, but broadly similar)
- **Feed pressure:** 53% of hay inventory and 52% of alfalfa acreage in drought areas
- **Cash cattle:** $253.275-258/cwt nationally; June futures up $1.82/week
- **Liquidation window:** ~2 weeks to late June/early July forced decisions (down from 3 weeks on June 4)
- **USDA forecast:** 2026 beef production lowered 243M lbs to 25.547B lbs on slower slaughter pace; 2027 prices projected at new highs as supply remains limited
- **Breeding genetics:** Drought forcing some ranching families to liquidate breeding stock refined over decades — irreversible multi-generational loss
- **Screwworm:** News impacting cattle futures volatility; some traders misinterpreting the signal
- **USDA disaster:** $18B disbursed; deadline extended to August

**Strategic Impact:** The 2-week window to forced liquidation decisions is the most critical near-term timeline in agriculture. Even without mass liquidation, USDA's forecast of lower 2026 production and record 2027 prices confirms the structural shortage thesis through at least 2028. The breeding genetics liquidation detail is the most concerning long-term signal — rebuilding herds takes 3-5 years under ideal conditions, and current conditions are far from ideal. The June WASDE (June 11) will provide the next official supply assessment.

### 2. HPAI — Texas Dairy Detection; Vaccine Trials Continue
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (APHIS, CDC, KBTX, USDA)

H5N1 update:

- **New detection:** Texas dairy herd confirmed positive (May 30) — first Texas dairy case of 2026
- **Spread:** Active across multiple states in poultry and dairy cattle
- **Vaccine:** USDA supporting rapid development; multiple candidates in field trials. Earlier Nature study (June 4 briefing) showed cross-species protection in mouse models
- **Surveillance:** National Milk Testing Strategy implemented for comprehensive H5N1 monitoring
- **Human cases:** CDC maintains "low" public health risk assessment; cases limited to dairy/poultry worker exposures
- **Food safety:** Pasteurization neutralizes the virus; milk supply safe
- **California:** CDFA quarantines previously lifted; state in Stage 3 monitoring

**Strategic Impact:** The Texas dairy detection is notable as the first 2026 case in the state, suggesting the virus remains active despite improved biosecurity measures. The vaccine timeline (field trials ongoing, potential availability late 2026 or early 2027) remains the key variable for transitioning from reactive to proactive HPAI management. No escalation in severity.

## Crops

### 3. June WASDE Due June 11 — Critical Report for Corn, Wheat, Soybeans
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, CME, Pro Farmer)

The June WASDE report releases in 3 days and will be the most closely watched report of the season:

- **Corn conditions:** 67% good/excellent (inaugural rating, in line with 40-year average); spring wheat 47% G/E; winter wheat only 26% G/E (44% very poor/poor)
- **Corn production:** 16.0B bushels projected, down 6% YoY on lower acreage (95.3M acres, -3.5M)
- **Winter wheat:** First survey-based forecast down 25% YoY to 1.048B bushels; Hard Red Winter down sharply
- **Soybeans:** Projected at 4.435B bushels, up 173M from 2025 on higher harvested area and trend yield
- **June Agricultural Survey:** USDA contacting 90,000+ producers for acreage and stock data; results published June 30 (Acreage, Grain Stocks, livestock reports)
- **Pollination watch:** Corn pollination window (late June through July) remains the make-or-break period

**Strategic Impact:** The June WASDE will either confirm or revise the May projections based on emerging harvest and weather data. Winter wheat at 26% G/E vs. the already-low production forecast suggests further downward revision is possible. Corn's 67% G/E is adequate but offers no buffer. The June 30 Acreage and Grain Stocks reports will provide the definitive picture for the 2026/27 marketing year.

### 4. USDA Announces $60M Small Meat Processor Investment + Specialty Crop Program
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, FSA)

Two significant USDA programs launched:

- **Small Processors Action Plan:** Secretary Rollins launched $60M investment to support small and very small meat/poultry processing plants (June 3). Reduces regulatory burdens and improves support infrastructure
- **Specialty Crop Assistance (ASCF):** $1.625B in payments to eligible specialty crop producers for elevated input costs and market disruptions. Login.gov applications since June 1; in-person enrollment starts today (June 8)
- **June lending rates:** USDA FSA announced June 2026 lending rates for agricultural producers

**Strategic Impact:** The Small Processors Action Plan addresses a structural bottleneck — small meat processors were overwhelmed during COVID and remain constrained. Expanding capacity diversifies the processing supply chain away from concentration in Tyson/JBS/Cargill. The $1.625B specialty crop program is significant support for producers facing input cost inflation and trade disruption.

## Gardening

### 5. Zone 6a/6b (Hermitage, PA): Second Week of June — Rainfall Relief Expected
**Heat:** Low | **Momentum:** SEASONAL | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Drought.gov, NOAA, Weather forecasts)

Updated PA conditions:

- **PA drought:** ~20% under active drought (down from ~22% on June 4); 10% abnormally dry
- **Rainfall outlook:** Significant rainfall expected through June — over 200mm projected for the month. Multiple rainy days forecast
- **Temperatures:** Highs ~82F / lows ~64F — ideal growing conditions

**This week's priorities:**
- Deep water only if rainfall disappoints; mulch all beds 2-3" to retain incoming moisture
- Succession sow beans, lettuce, radish
- Side-dress tomatoes and corn with nitrogen after rain
- **ACTIVE PEST WINDOW:** Squash vine borer adults still flying — apply Bt or row cover BEFORE boring. This remains the #1 preventable summer loss
- Spotted lanternfly nymphs: Check tree of heaven, grape, ornamentals
- Japanese beetles arriving; cucumber beetles active on cucurbits
- **Harvest:** Strawberries (peak winding down), lettuce, radish, peas, garlic scapes
- **Plan:** Start fall brassica seeds indoors by late June (2-3 weeks away)
- **ASCF program:** If you grow specialty crops commercially, in-person enrollment starts TODAY (June 8)

**Strategic Impact:** The rainfall forecast is the best weather signal for PA gardeners this season. If June delivers 200mm+, soil moisture recovery will support warm-season crops through pollination and early fruit set. Squash vine borer prevention remains time-critical.
