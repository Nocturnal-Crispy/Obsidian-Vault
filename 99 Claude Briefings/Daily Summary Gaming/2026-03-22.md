# Gaming Intelligence — Sunday, March 22, 2026

## Story 1: Slay the Spire 2 Sets New Peak — 574K Concurrent Players
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

Slay the Spire 2 has pushed past its initial launch peak to reach 574,638 concurrent Steam players, cementing its position in Steam's all-time top 20. The game maintains a daily average near 500K users and holds a 97% Overwhelmingly Positive rating on approximately 9,000 reviews. This is the biggest single-game launch on Steam in 2026.

For context, this is more than double Crimson Desert's 239K peak, demonstrating that a focused indie sequel with strong fundamentals can outperform AAA launches by massive margins.

**Why it matters:** Slay the Spire 2 is now the benchmark Steam launch of 2026. Its early access model, combined with a deeply loyal community, continues to validate the "proven indie → sequel" pipeline as the most reliable path to massive Steam success.

## Story 3: Crimson Desert — Post-Launch Stabilization, Reviews Trending Upward
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** DECLINING | **Source Tier:** 1 (Confirmed)

Crimson Desert's post-launch trajectory continues to stabilize. Steam reviews have trended from "Mixed" toward "Mostly Positive" with 16,000+ reviews, though the concurrent player count is settling well below Slay the Spire 2's dominance. Pearl Abyss's stock remains significantly depressed from the 30% crash on launch day. The company continues to ship rapid patches addressing the control scheme complaints.

**Why it matters:** Crimson Desert is following the classic "strong sales, disappointing reviews" trajectory. The key question is whether Pearl Abyss can iterate fast enough to convert the 2M+ buyers into long-term players before the next wave of major releases.
