### US Commerce Department Withdraws AI Chip Export Control Draft Rule
**Heat: High**
**Trend: New Signal**

The Commerce Department withdrew a draft regulation that would have required foreign nations to invest in American data centers or provide security assurances to receive exports of 200,000+ AI chips. The rule — intended to replace the January 2025 Biden-era AI diffusion regulation — was pulled from interagency review on Friday, March 14. The department has stated it will not return to the Biden approach, instead favoring deal-by-deal arrangements like those with Saudi Arabia and the UAE.

**What changed:** The regulatory landscape for AI chip exports has shifted from a rules-based framework toward bilateral deals. This removes a major uncertainty for NVIDIA, AMD, and their international customers.
**Who is affected:** NVIDIA, AMD, Intel (chip stocks rallied on the news); Middle East sovereign AI projects; Chinese chip importers; US allies seeking compute access.
**What to watch:** Whether deal-by-deal approach accelerates Gulf state AI investments; impact on US-China Paris trade talks; Congressional pushback on reduced export controls.

## GPU / Semiconductor

### AI Chip Supply Crisis — Export Rule Withdrawal Eases One Constraint
**Heat: High**
**Trend: Continuing Trend**
**Previous Coverage:** 2026-03-16 15:24

The withdrawal of the AI chip export draft rule removes one layer of supply uncertainty, but the physical supply crunch persists. GDDR7 and LPDDR5X memory shortages continue forcing chipmakers to prioritize AI accelerators over consumer hardware. Intel expects inventory to hit its lowest level this quarter but projects improvement in Q2. AMD's data center chief reaffirmed demand remains unprecedented. Intel stock surged 3.7% and AMD gained nearly 1% on Monday, partly driven by the export rule news.

**What changed:** Regulatory overhang lifted. Physical supply constraints remain the binding constraint.
**Who is affected:** Consumer GPU buyers (continued shortage risk), data center operators, memory manufacturers.
**What to watch:** Q2 memory pricing trends; Intel 18A production ramp; whether NVIDIA GeForce production cuts materialize; consumer GPU pricing trajectory.
