# Tech Intelligence — Wednesday, April 29, 2026

## 1. Microsoft-OpenAI Exclusivity Dissolved — GPT-5.5 Lands on AWS Bedrock Day One
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (CNBC, Amazon Blog, TechCrunch, The Decoder)**

Microsoft and OpenAI announced a fundamental restructuring of their landmark partnership, ending the exclusivity arrangement that had defined the AI cloud landscape since 2023. Microsoft's license to OpenAI's IP transitions from exclusive to non-exclusive through 2032, freeing OpenAI to offer all products on any cloud provider. Within 24 hours, AWS rolled out GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.4 on Amazon Bedrock alongside a jointly built agent service and Amazon Bedrock Managed Agents powered by OpenAI. The financial terms were also revised: Microsoft stops paying revenue share to OpenAI, while OpenAI continues paying Microsoft a capped share through 2030. The restructuring is widely seen as pre-IPO housecleaning — providing clarity for investors about OpenAI's long-term obligations. Strategic implication: this is the most significant cloud partnership reshuffling since Google lost its Android exclusivity leverage. Anthropic and Google now face a multi-cloud OpenAI competing for every enterprise contract.

## 2. Musk v. Altman Trial Enters Day 3 — $134B and OpenAI's Future at Stake
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (CNBC, CNN, NPR, Washington Post)**

The trial between Elon Musk and OpenAI CEO Sam Altman entered its third day in federal court in Oakland before Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers and a nine-person jury. Musk is seeking reversion of OpenAI to nonprofit structure, removal of Altman and Brockman from the board, and up to $134 billion in "wrongful gains" returned to the nonprofit foundation. In testimony, Musk claimed he "came up with the idea, the name, recruited the key people" and would not have contributed if the founders intended to profit. He said he wasn't opposed to a small for-profit subsidiary "as long as the tail didn't wag the dog." OpenAI's lead attorney William Savitt countered that Musk is simply a competitor trying to attack OpenAI, and that Musk himself pushed for a for-profit structure but left when he couldn't exert total control. The judge reprimanded both Musk and Altman for social media posts during proceedings. Jared Birchall, who manages Musk's family office and serves as an xAI executive, is expected to testify next. The verdict will shape OpenAI's corporate structure, its IPO timeline, and the competitive dynamics of the entire AI industry.

## 3. GPT-5.5 Released — OpenAI's Fastest Model Cadence in History
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (OpenAI Blog, CNBC, TechCrunch, Fortune)**

OpenAI released GPT-5.5 on April 23, just six weeks after GPT-5.4 — the fastest consecutive major model release in the company's history. The model rolled out to paid subscribers in ChatGPT and Codex, with API access following the next day. GPT-5.5 is described as excelling at code, research, data analysis, document creation, and multi-tool orchestration. It is also now generally available on GitHub Copilot. The rapid cadence signals OpenAI is accelerating its release tempo ahead of its IPO and in response to competitive pressure from Anthropic's Mythos and Google's Ironwood-powered inference. GPT-5.5 is already available on AWS Bedrock alongside the exclusivity dissolution.

## 4. Intel Q1 Earnings Blowout — Stock Surges 20%, Sparks Semiconductor Rally
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Intel IR, CNBC, Yahoo Finance)**

Intel reported Q1 2026 revenue of $13.6 billion and adjusted EPS of $0.29, both massively exceeding Wall Street estimates of $12.36B revenue and $0.01 EPS. The data center and AI division (DCAI) climbed 22% to $5.1 billion, with AI-driven businesses now representing 60% of Intel revenue, up 40% YoY. Intel guided Q2 revenue to $13.8B–$14.8B vs. consensus $13.07B. The stock surged 20%+ after-hours, triggering a broad semiconductor rally — the iShares Semiconductor ETF gained 40.4% in April. Intel also announced partnerships with Musk's Terafab facility and a multiyear Xeon CPU arrangement with Google Cloud. This is the strongest Intel earnings beat in over a decade and signals the company is finally converting its foundry investments into data center AI revenue.

## 5. Nvidia Crosses $5 Trillion Market Cap — World's Most Valuable Company
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (CNBC, Yahoo Finance, Intellectia)**

Nvidia closed at a record $208.27 on April 24, pushing its market cap past $5.12 trillion and cementing its position as the world's most valuable company — $1 trillion ahead of second-place Alphabet. The stock gained 19% in April alone, fueled by Intel's earnings beat sparking sector-wide optimism, a confirmed $1 trillion backlog (Blackwell sold out through mid-2026, Vera Rubin already allocated), and cooling Iran tensions at mid-month. The $5T milestone makes Nvidia the first semiconductor company to reach this valuation. With Vera Rubin entering full allocation and next-generation Rubin Ultra in design, Nvidia's infrastructure dominance continues to compound.

## 6. Anthropic Mythos: NSA Using It Despite Pentagon Blacklist — White House Drafting Federal Access
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 2 (Axios, Bloomberg, The Hill, CyberScoop)**

The White House Office of Management and Budget is drafting guidance to let federal agencies access Anthropic's Mythos model, even as the Department of Defense maintains its "supply chain risk" designation. Axios reported that the NSA is already using Mythos Preview despite the Pentagon blacklist — a striking split within the defense establishment. The Treasury Department has also requested access. OpenAI and Anthropic both briefed the House Homeland Security Committee behind closed doors on cybersecurity threats this week. The Mythos model identified thousands of previously unknown, high-severity vulnerabilities across major operating systems and browsers during testing. The federal AI landscape is now fractured: the Pentagon says Anthropic is a risk, while the NSA and civilian agencies are racing to adopt its most capable model.
