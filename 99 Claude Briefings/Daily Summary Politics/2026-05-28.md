## Iran-US Strait of Hormuz Crisis — Ceasefire Violations
**Heat: Critical | Momentum: RISING | Tier 1 — Confirmed**

Iran fired a ballistic missile at a US air base in Kuwait on Thursday, hours after US strikes on Iran. Kuwait intercepted the missile with no casualties. Both sides accuse each other of violating the shaky ceasefire. Iran targeted a US base it says was responsible for strikes on Bandar Abbas (Iranian port near Strait of Hormuz).

President Trump is reviewing a draft peace deal: a 60-day phased plan where Iran loosens its Hormuz grip and the US pulls back its naval blockade in synchronized steps. The Strait has been largely blocked since February 28, 2026, when the US and Israel launched air operations against Iran.

**Strategic Impact:** Oil prices spiking on renewed fears. A 60-day synchronized withdrawal would be unprecedented. Failure risks broader Gulf escalation.

## Ken Paxton Defeats John Cornyn — Texas Senate GOP Runoff
**Heat: High | Momentum: NEW | Tier 1 — Confirmed**

Texas AG Ken Paxton won the Republican primary runoff with 63.8% on May 26, ending Sen. John Cornyn's 24-year Senate career. Cornyn is the first Republican Texas senator to lose a party nomination for reelection. Trump's eleventh-hour endorsement (one week before election day) provided decisive momentum. General election matchup: Paxton vs. Democrat James Talarico (Austin state rep).

**Strategic Impact:** Signals continued rightward shift in Texas GOP. Trump endorsement power remains decisive in primaries. Cornyn's loss removes a Senate institutionalist.
