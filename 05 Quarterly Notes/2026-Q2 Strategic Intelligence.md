# Q2 2026 Strategic Intelligence Report

## Quarter Overview

Q2 2026 was the quarter the AI industry's center of gravity moved from *model capability* to *compute economics and capital markets*. Custom AI silicon went mainstream — OpenAI's Broadcom-built "Jalapeño" inference chip (targeting ~50% cheaper inference) joined Google's TPUs, Meta's MTIA, and reportedly Anthropic, Apple, and Fujitsu, cementing Broadcom as the custom-silicon kingmaker. Both leading labs moved toward public markets (Anthropic confidential IPO filing; OpenAI IPO prep), signaling a durable, well-capitalized foundation for builders. On the macro side, the spring energy shock unwound: WTI fell from ~$113 in April to ~$76 by quarter-end following a provisional US–Iran de-escalation, while the Fed held a hawkish 3.50–3.75% with sticky core PCE near 3.6%. For the user's two hats, the quarter's most material throughlines were falling AI inference costs (good for software margins), easing fuel and feed-grain costs (good for farm margins), firm cattle prices, and the OBBBA 1099 threshold rising to $2,000 for 2026.

## Industry Power Shifts

**AI Compute** — The battlefield decisively shifted to "who runs models cheapest and fastest." Custom inference silicon proliferated (OpenAI Jalapeño; Amazon custom-chip run-rate reportedly >$20B), pressuring per-token pricing downward across providers — a structural tailwind for independent developers building AI features into client apps.

**AI Models & Capital** — The Anthropic/OpenAI rivalry matured from product competition into a capital-markets race, with both pursuing IPOs. New flagship models (GPT-5.5/5.6 generation; Anthropic Opus 4.8) pushed agentic-coding benchmarks higher. Reported valuations varied widely across outlets and remain unconfirmed.

**Developer Tooling** — Agentic coding tools kept maturing; Cognition's "Devin Fusion" (late June) is the latest entrant, raising the bar (and the eval burden) for solo consultants choosing a stack.

**Security Landscape** — A steady drumbeat of actively-exploited CISA KEV additions hit small-shop infrastructure: Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager (CVE-2026-20245, then -20262), SolarWinds Serv-U, Splunk Enterprise, Ubiquiti UniFi OS, and wormable Microsoft June Patch Tuesday RCEs. Patch cadence and asset inventory remained the recurring lesson for client environments.

## Agriculture & Commodity-Cycle Shifts

The US cattle-herd contraction kept live/feeder cattle firm through the quarter (a sellers' market), while lean hogs stayed soft (~$92.52 at June expiry). Soft corn (~$4.13) and soybeans (~$11.26) improved feed/input margins for livestock. Falling fuel eased operating and transport costs. The quarter closed on the June 30 USDA Acreage + Grain Stocks double-release, the key data point setting new-crop direction into harvest. HPAI/H5N1 remained a low-level background biosecurity risk heading toward fall. A late-June Heat Advisory (HI 104–106°F) underscored summer heat-stress management for both livestock and garden.

## Policy Shifts (two-hat relevance)

The OBBBA 1099 regime took effect for 2026: 1099-MISC/NEC threshold $600 → $2,000 (inflation-indexed from 2027); 1099-K reverted to $20,000 / 200 transactions. Emerging AI hiring-tool bias-audit rules (NYC-style) began surfacing as a small-business compliance area relevant to AI integration work.

## Grant-Landscape Shifts

A quiet quarter for open windows. USDA REAP paused since 03-31; VAPG and NE SARE Farmer Grant closed (watch FY2027). SBIR/STTR was reauthorized through 2031 (S.3971, signed 04-13), but the USDA SBIR Phase I FY2026 NOFO had not yet posted by quarter-end — the single most important grants watch item heading into Q3.

## Gaming

A heavy reveal-and-launch stretch: Summer Game Fest and the June 8 Xbox Games Showcase (Halo: Campaign Evolved, Gears of War: E-Day); Final Fantasy VII Rebirth and Star Fox on Switch 2; the Steam Machine launch. Q3 opens with Palworld 1.0 (July 10) and Halo: Campaign Evolved (July).

## Local & Regional (Mercer County / Hermitage)

Mercer County's 2026 property reassessment values moved to the Tax Assessment Office (watch for tax-bill impact and appeal windows). Hermitage advanced community projects (Stull Nature Reserve Council Ring + Environmental Learning Pavilion; summer stormwater work). Watch items carried into Q3: a pending data-center zoning ordinance vote and an August county judicial transition.

## Defining Trends of Q2 2026

1. AI competition shifts from model quality to inference cost and capital formation.
2. Custom silicon (Broadcom as kingmaker) breaks the GPU-only assumption for inference.
3. Both frontier labs head for public markets — durability for builders.
4. Energy shock unwinds (WTI ~$113 → ~$76); Fed stays hawkish on sticky inflation.
5. Cattle firm / hogs soft / feed grains cheap — improving livestock margins.
6. Relentless CISA KEV cadence keeps patch discipline the #1 SMB security task.
7. OBBBA 1099 thresholds reshape contractor/marketplace reporting for 2026.

## Strategic Outlook — Q3 2026

- **Agriculture:** Trade the June 30 USDA data into a marketing plan; lock favorable feed/input pricing while grains stay soft; maintain heat-stress and fall HPAI vigilance.
- **Grants:** Prioritize catching the USDA SBIR FY2026 NOFO (ag-tech ties both hats); monitor REAP for any unpause.
- **Local:** Track your reassessment notice and the Hermitage data-center zoning decision.
