## 3. Crazy Taxi: World Tour — Announced and Immediately Mired in AI Controversy
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Kotaku, PC Gamer, Push Square, Game Informer, Shacknews)

SEGA's Crazy Taxi revival launched into a controversy within hours of its reveal:

- **The game:** Open-world reboot by original creator Kenji Kanno. Five cities, campaign + arcade + online multiplayer. The Offspring's "All I Want" in the trailer. 2027 release on PS5, Xbox, PC, Switch 2
- **The controversy:** Steam page includes a Generative AI Content Disclosure. SEGA confirmed AI was used for "background assets" during development — "just for ideas and as a reference"
- **Community reaction:** Immediate backlash. Critics argue any generative AI use undermines artist labor and constitutes "theft." Positive reveal hype significantly dampened
- **Denuvo:** Also confirmed, adding a second friction point with PC players

**Strategic Impact:** This is a case study in how AI disclosure requirements (Steam now mandates them) create instant PR risk even when the AI use is limited. SEGA's explanation — AI for background asset ideation — is arguably the least intrusive possible use, yet the backlash is treating it as equivalent to full AI-generated art. The combination of AI disclosure + Denuvo DRM on a nostalgia-driven revival is a messaging disaster. Other studios are watching closely — this will influence whether publishers disclose early or try to minimize AI use entirely.
