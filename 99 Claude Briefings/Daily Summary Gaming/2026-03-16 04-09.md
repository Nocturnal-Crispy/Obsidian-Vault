### WWE 2K26 Review Bombed Over Ringside Pass Microtransactions
**Heat: Medium**
**Trend: New Trend**

WWE 2K26 (launched March 13) is being aggressively review-bombed on Steam, sitting just above 50% — approaching the infamy of WWE 2K20. The core complaint is the Ringside Pass, a Battle Pass system that locks previously standard DLC content behind both a purchase and a grind. Skip Tiers cost $1.99 each, or $80 to skip all tiers on top of the base game. Critics praise the in-ring wrestling mechanics as the best in the series, but user sentiment is overwhelmingly negative on monetization.

**What changed:** A major sports game launch is being actively rejected by its community over aggressive monetization, despite strong gameplay fundamentals.
**Who is affected:** 2K Games/Visual Concepts, WWE gaming franchise reputation, players of sports simulation games.
**What to watch:** Whether 2K adjusts the Ringside Pass pricing or structure; impact on first-month sales vs. previous entries; whether this affects other sports game publishers' monetization strategies.
