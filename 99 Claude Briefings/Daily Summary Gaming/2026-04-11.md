# Gaming Intelligence — Saturday, April 11, 2026

## 1. Hades II Console Launch — 3 Days Out, PC Base Holding Low Before Pull-In
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (Supergiant, Xbox Wire, Pure Xbox)**

Hades II ships on PS5, Xbox Series X|S and Cloud on Tuesday, April 14, day-one on Game Pass Ultimate, Premium and PC — with Xbox release times now published by Pure Xbox ahead of the launch window. Current Steam concurrency is roughly 8,000 peak over the last 24 hours, well below the ~112.9K all-time peak set at full launch in September 2025. That is the expected pre-console trough: committed PC players have already finished their builds, casual-returning players are waiting for the console/Game Pass onramp, and the Tuesday launch will compress both audiences back into the concurrent graph. Watch for two signals on launch day: (1) whether combined PC + Game Pass CCU exceeds the original 112K peak, which would confirm the Game Pass pull-in thesis; and (2) whether Xbox Play Anywhere cross-save adoption pulls players off Switch, where Hades II spent six months as a console exclusive.

## 2. Pragmata — 6 Days Out, All Platforms Same Day, Switch 2 Gets Its First AAA Anchor
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (Capcom), Tier 2 (PC Gamer, TweakTown)**

Pragmata is locked for April 17 on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC (Steam) and Nintendo Switch 2 in most regions — Japan and Asia on Switch 2 get April 24. Capcom moved the release a full week earlier because "April is otherwise wide open" — a meaningful confidence signal from a publisher that has whiffed dates before with this title. GeForce Now is adding Pragmata to its cloud library at launch. For the Switch 2 calendar, this is the platform's highest-profile multiplatform AAA of April and the clearest test of whether third-party publishers will treat Switch 2 as a true launch-day platform rather than a four-week later port target. Extended previews remain firmly positive on the combat-hacking dual loop and the ~2M pre-release demo downloads give it real awareness. Post-Monster Hunter Wilds / RE Requiem, Pragmata is Capcom's 2026 "new IP" swing.

## 3. Game Pass April Wave — Catalog Density Ahead of Hades II Tuesday
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Xbox Wire, GameSpot, Kotaku)**

The April Game Pass wave is running through its pre-Hades II rollout: Planet Coaster 2 (April 9) and Tiny Bookshop (April 10) are live. Wave 2 includes Oblivion Remastered, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare (the 2019 one), Kiln, Vampire Crawlers and more — bringing the April catalog total to 17+ titles, five of which are day-one additions. Coming into the Hades II Tuesday drop, Game Pass has its strongest single-month lineup since late 2024 and continues to widen the perceived value gap versus PlayStation Plus, which has not responded publicly to the Sony dynamic-pricing backlash that started in late March. REPLACED — the long-delayed 2.5D side-scroller — also ships Tuesday alongside Hades II as a Game Pass day-one, testing whether double-A Game Pass launches can survive sharing a release window with a flagship.

## 4. Steam Weekend Tops — CS2 / Dota 2 Stable, Wishlist Sales Activity Strong
**Heat: Low | Momentum: SUSTAINED | Source: Tier 1 (Steam, SteamDB), Tier 3 (community activity)**

Steam's top-of-chart remains fundamentally unchanged: Counter-Strike 2 anchoring the #1 slot, Dota 2 holding steady behind it, and the usual PUBG / Rust / Team Fortress 2 cluster. Wishlist-sale activity is high — Steam emailed notifications on Tales of ARISE and WUCHANG: Fallen Feathers dropping this week, consistent with a pre-Hades II "clear your backlog" merchandising push. No new indie breakouts to report today; the April 11 weekend is functioning as a holding pattern before the April 14 / 17 double-drop. ARC Raiders remains the game to watch for a cycle-start content drop; Hollow Knight Silksong and Slay the Spire 2 both continue steady long-tail numbers off their March peaks.
