# Weekly Intelligence Rollup — Week 13 (March 23–29, 2026)

## Executive Summary

Week 13 was defined by three converging crises: the Iran war's escalation from air campaign to potential ground operations, the DHS shutdown crossing into its sixth week with constitutional questions arising from executive action on TSA pay, and the largest single-day protest in American history. In tech, the AI IPO race crystallized as a three-way contest between Anthropic, OpenAI, and SpaceX-xAI. AMD's $60B Meta deal cracked Nvidia's monopoly narrative. In gaming, Sony's dynamic pricing controversy entered a sustained phase without company response.

## Technology

### Week's Biggest Story: The AI IPO Race
Anthropic announced IPO exploration at a $380B valuation targeting October 2026, one day after winning a landmark injunction against the DOD's supply-chain designation. OpenAI targets Q4 2026 at potentially $1T. SpaceX-xAI's merged entity targets up to $1.5T. Combined, these could raise $150B+ — the largest IPO wave in tech history. Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, and Morgan Stanley are competing for lead roles.

### Anthropic v. DOD: From Crisis to Triumph
The week's most dramatic arc: Monday's hearing before Judge Rita Lin → Wednesday's preliminary injunction win → Thursday's IPO signal. Anthropic's defense of ethical red lines (no mass surveillance, no autonomous weapons) was validated by the courts and rewarded by the market. This sequence may define the relationship between AI companies and government for the next decade.

### AMD Breaks Nvidia's Data Center Monopoly
Meta awarded AMD a ~$60B, five-year deal for custom MI450 GPUs and EPYC "Venice" CPUs — the largest chip deal in history. AMD stock is up 88% YoY vs Nvidia's 46%. Samsung HBM4 collaboration and a multi-billion Seoul AI partnership further expanded AMD's footprint.

### Inference Economics Revolution
Google's TurboQuant (6x memory compression, no accuracy loss) rattled memory stocks (Micron -3%, WDC -4.7%, SanDisk -5.7%). Nvidia's Vera Rubin entered full production (3.5x training, 5x inference vs Blackwell). Google released Gemini Flash-Lite at $0.25/M tokens. The combined effect: AI inference costs are falling at a rate that could unlock new application categories.

### Other Notable Signals
- Jensen Huang declared "we've achieved AGI" at GTC, then qualified it — generated debate about definition thresholds
- DOJ $2.5B AI chip smuggling cases exposed state-level evasion sophistication
- SoftBank secured $40B bridge loan for further OpenAI investment
- Arm launched AGI CPU architecture
- LiteLLM supply chain attack via TeamPCP — critical security incident
- OpenAI shut down Sora; Disney $1B deal collapsed
- China's AI industry pivoting from models to agents (Zhongguancun Forum)
- Apple Siri opening to third-party AI in iOS 27

### Week-over-Week Trend Changes
**Accelerating:** AI IPO pipeline, AMD market share gains, inference cost compression
**Stable:** Nvidia generational cadence, TSMC manufacturing moat
**Fading:** DOJ chip smuggling reverberations, Sora aftermath

Sony's algorithmic pricing system — showing different players different prices for the same game — expanded to US during the Spring Sale (189 games, through April 22). The story sustained across the full week without any Sony response. Push Square poll: 50% call it "unfair." European DMA regulators have not yet commented. This is the most significant pricing story in gaming since loot box legislation.

## Politics

### Week's Biggest Story: Iran War Escalates Toward Ground Operations
The Iran war progressed from Day 24 to Day 30 with dramatic escalation at every level:

- **Military:** Air campaign continued (nuclear sites struck at Arak, Ardakan, Bushehr). Houthis entered the war with missile strikes on Israel — dual-chokepoint threat (Bab al-Mandeb + Hormuz). Pentagon now preparing potential ground operations including Kharg Island seizure. US soldiers injured in Houthi attack on Saudi base.
- **Diplomatic:** Iran rejected 15-point peace plan, set 5 counter-conditions. Pakistan, Turkey, Egypt, Saudi Arabia meeting in Islamabad for de-escalation. Trump's April 6 energy-strike pause deadline approaching. Witkoff expects meetings with Iran "this week."
- **Humanitarian:** Lebanon displacement crisis at 1M+. Deaths reported across UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, West Bank, Oman, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia. Iran internet blackout at 30 days (696+ hours).

### "No Kings" Protests — March 28
Estimated 8-9 million participants across 3,300+ events in all 50 states — largest single-day protest in US history. Flagship rally in Minneapolis-St. Paul with Bruce Springsteen. Nearly half of events in Republican strongholds. Multiple grievance convergence: war, ICE killings, cost of living, shutdown.

### DHS Shutdown: Week 6
The shutdown progressed from Day 39 to Day 44 with no resolution:
- Senate passed fund-everything-except-ICE/CBP bill (unanimous)
- House rejected it, passed own 60-day stopgap including ICE/CBP
- Senate declared House bill dead on arrival
- Trump signed executive order to pay TSA via reconciliation funds (constitutionally questionable)
- TSA damage: 480+ officers quit, 40-50% callout rates, 4.5-hour waits
- Trump linked Iran war to DHS funding as political leverage

### CPAC 2026
Generational MAGA split over Iran war documented across three days. Younger conservatives expressed "betrayal" over interventionism. Trump and Vance absent throughout. Story declining as "No Kings" protests dominate coverage.

### Other Notable Signals
- Trump criticized NATO response to Iran war
- Rubio at G7 — European allies cautious on war conduct
- Tomahawk depletion concerns reported
- Three Democrats crossed party lines on House DHS bill (Davis, Gluesenkamp Perez, Cuellar)

### Week-over-Week Trend Changes
**Accelerating:** Iran war escalation (air → potential ground), anti-war protest movement, DHS constitutional crisis

**Fading:** CPAC coverage, initial DHS deal optimism
