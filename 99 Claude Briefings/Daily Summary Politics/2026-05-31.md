## 1. Iran Ceasefire Extension: Still Awaiting Trump Approval — Day 3 of Limbo
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (CNN, CBS, Congress.gov CRS)

The tentative US-Iran 60-day ceasefire extension remains unsigned. Key status:

- Negotiators reached agreement May 28 on extending the ceasefire and reopening the Strait of Hormuz
- Iran must remove sea mines within 30 days, surrender highly enriched uranium, and commit to no nuclear weapons
- Trump "recently edited" the agreement text (per CBS sources), suggesting active engagement but no finalization
- Ceasefire is on "life support" per Trump's own characterization
- Intermittent fighting has continued since May 4 despite the April 8 ceasefire
- Treasury Secretary Bessent said sanctions relief would "go very slowly"

Timeline: Original ceasefire brokered by Pakistan April 8, extended indefinitely April 21, but fighting resumed May 4.

**Strategic Impact:** Every day without signature increases escalation risk. Hormuz closure continues to elevate global shipping costs, energy prices, and fertilizer supply chains. A collapse would spike oil prices and agricultural input costs immediately.

## 2. Section 122 Tariffs: 54 Days to Expiration — No Congressional Action
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USTR, court filings)

Section 122 tariffs expire July 24 unless Congress acts. No legislation has been introduced. The administration continues rebuilding tariff authority through Section 301/232 investigations covering 75%+ of imports after IEEPA and 122 legal setbacks.

China's $17B annual agricultural purchase target has been downgraded to a "guiding target" by China's Commerce Ministry — effectively removing the commitment. US-China trade uncertainty continues to cap grain export upside.

**Strategic Impact:** The July 24 cliff creates binary outcomes: either tariffs lapse (disrupting supply chains that adapted to them) or emergency legislation preserves them (political cost). Agricultural exporters face continued uncertainty regardless.

## 3. Trump Base Erosion: Non-College White Voters Net-Negative — Week 2
**Heat:** Medium | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 2 (Polling data, multiple outlets)

White voters without college degrees remain net-negative on Trump's job approval entering a second week. This demographic was the foundation of 2024 victory margins in MI, WI, PA. The erosion correlates with tariff-driven price increases on consumer goods and agricultural product uncertainty.

**Strategic Impact:** If durable into summer, this undermines GOP positioning in competitive House districts. Midterm implications grow as candidates begin distancing calculations. Watch for tariff messaging shifts.
