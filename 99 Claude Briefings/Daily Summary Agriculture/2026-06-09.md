## Livestock

### 1. Drought-Cattle Crisis — August Live Cattle Down $4.92; Liquidation Window ~1–2 Weeks
**Heat:** Critical | **Momentum:** SUSTAINED | **Source:** Tier 1 (USDA, CME, Brownfield Ag News, Drovers, AgAmerica)

The cattle market showed significant pressure today:

- **August live cattle:** Closed at $236.72, down $4.92 on June 8 — notable pullback from recent highs
- **Cash cattle:** $253-258/cwt range nationally (unchanged from yesterday)
- **Drought:** 58% of lower 48 in drought; 57% of cattle inventory in moderate drought or worse; 69% of winter wheat in drought
- **Liquidation window:** ~1-2 weeks to forced decisions (narrowing from 2 weeks yesterday)
- **Herd size:** Lowest in 75 years after years of drought-driven liquidation
- **Rebuild signals:** 18% YoY reduction in beef cow slaughter through first 14 weeks of 2026 — herd rebuilding intentions emerging, but slow and cautious
- **USDA forecast:** 2026 beef production lowered to 25.547B lbs; 2027 record highs projected
- **USDA disaster:** $18B disbursed; deadline extended to August
- **Virginia report:** Hay yields at 1-1.5 bales/acre vs. normal 4-5 — drought reducing feed supply dramatically

**Strategic Impact:** The August futures pullback ($236.72, -$4.92) is notable but likely reflects near-term position adjustments ahead of the June WASDE (2 days away), not a fundamental shift. The structural shortage thesis remains intact: 75-year low herd size, 18% reduced cow slaughter suggesting rebuilding attempts, but tight calf supplies for years to come. The Virginia hay yield data (70-75% below normal) illustrates why feed costs remain the binding constraint. The WASDE on June 11 will either confirm or challenge current production forecasts.

### 2. HPAI — 13 States with Dairy Herd Infections; Mortality Reports Emerging
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1 (APHIS, CDC, KBTX, FDA, WOAH)

H5N1 situation has expanded:

- **Geographic spread:** HPAI H5N1 now confirmed in dairy herds across 13 states — South Dakota, Michigan, Texas, Ohio, Colorado, Iowa, Minnesota, Wyoming, Oklahoma, and others
- **Mortality:** Dairy cow deaths reported in 5 states with estimated mortality rate up to 10% — a significant escalation from "low mortality" prior assessments
- **Texas:** First 2026 dairy case confirmed May 30
- **Vaccine:** Multiple candidates in field trials; USDA supporting rapid development. Potential availability late 2026 or early 2027
- **Surveillance:** National Milk Testing Strategy in place; USDA April 2024 Federal Order requiring pre-movement testing continues
- **Human risk:** CDC maintains "low" public health risk; cases limited to worker exposures
- **Food safety:** Pasteurization neutralizes the virus; milk supply safe

**Strategic Impact:** The expansion to 13 states and mortality reports up to 10% represent a meaningful escalation from yesterday's reporting. This is no longer "sporadic detections" — it's systematic spread. The mortality rate, if sustained, could affect dairy supply and pricing. The vaccine timeline (late 2026/early 2027) means 6+ months of reactive management ahead. The pre-movement testing order has slowed interstate spread but clearly hasn't contained it.
