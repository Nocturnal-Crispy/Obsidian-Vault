## 1. OpenAI Publishes Frontier Governance Framework
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (OpenAI blog)

OpenAI released its Frontier Governance Framework on May 29, mapping how its safety and security practices align with emerging legal requirements — specifically California's Transparency in Frontier AI Act and the EU AI Act's Code of Practice for General Purpose AI.

The framework covers risk assessment and mitigation across cyber offense, CBRN risks, harmful manipulation, and loss of control. It also addresses model reporting, security risk management, incident response, and external expert input. The existing Preparedness Framework remains the operational foundation for managing the most serious risks; this new document translates those practices into a public governance document tied to specific regulatory obligations.

**Strategic Impact:** This is OpenAI's most explicit regulatory compliance document to date. It positions the company ahead of enforcement timelines and signals that voluntary governance frameworks are becoming de facto regulatory filings. Other frontier labs will face pressure to publish comparable documents.

## 2. Microsoft Exchange CVE-2026-42897 Actively Exploited via Crafted Email
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (The Hacker News, CVE databases)

A spoofing vulnerability in on-prem Microsoft Exchange Server (CVE-2026-42897, CVSS 8.1) is being actively weaponized. Attackers send crafted emails that, when opened in Outlook Web Access, execute arbitrary JavaScript in the browser context. The flaw stems from a cross-site scripting bug.

Additionally, FortiClient EMS CVE-2026-35616 (CVSS 9.1) — a pre-authentication API access bypass — is under active exploitation to deliver credential-stealing malware. Microsoft's May Patch Tuesday fixed 120 vulnerabilities including 29 critical RCE flaws; the Windows DNS Client RCE (CVE-2026-41096) and Netlogon RCE (CVE-2026-41089) are the highest-priority patches.

**Strategic Impact:** Exchange Server on-prem deployments are under direct threat. Patch immediately. The FortiClient EMS exploitation targets endpoint management infrastructure — a particularly dangerous attack surface.

## 3. Steam Deck Prices Hiked Up to 50% on Global Memory Shortage
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** NEW | **Source:** Tier 1 (Valve, Tom's Hardware, Engadget)

Valve raised Steam Deck OLED prices by up to $300 effective May 27: the 1TB model jumped from $649 to $949 and the 512GB from $549 to $789. Valve cited rising component costs — DRAM, NAND flash, and GPU silicon — driven by AI infrastructure demand absorbing global memory supply.

This is the first major price increase since the Steam Deck launched at $399 in 2022. Nintendo has similarly raised hardware prices in 2026, pointing to an industry-wide squeeze. Refurbished LCD models remain at $359.

**Strategic Impact:** The AI compute buildout is now visibly impacting consumer electronics pricing. Memory allocation to AI training/inference is crowding out gaming hardware. This validates Nvidia's decision to skip new gaming GPUs in 2026 — there simply isn't enough memory to go around.

## 4. Nvidia Vera CPU Goes Standalone: Eyes $200B Server CPU Market
**Heat:** High | **Momentum:** RISING | **Source:** Tier 1/2 (Nvidia earnings, Motley Fool)

Nvidia announced it will offer its next-generation Vera server CPU as a standalone product, targeting the broader $200 billion server CPU market rather than bundling it exclusively with GPU systems. Every major hyperscaler and system maker is partnering for Vera deployment, with Nvidia projecting $20 billion in CPU revenue by 2027.

Meanwhile, AMD's position strengthened with its $60 billion, 6-gigawatt Meta partnership (announced February) now entering deployment phase — custom MI450 GPUs begin shipping H2 2026. AMD's AI accelerator market share is projected to climb from ~9% to 15%+ by year-end.

**Strategic Impact:** Nvidia is now competing directly with Intel and AMD in the server CPU market, not just GPU. This opens a new front in the data center war. Combined with AMD's Meta deal gaining traction, the compute landscape is diversifying faster than expected.
