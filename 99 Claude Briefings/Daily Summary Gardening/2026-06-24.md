## 🐛 Pest & disease watch

#### Squash vine borer adult flight peaks now
`Heat: Medium` · `Momentum: RISING` · `Tier 2 — Reputable` · `Category: Gardening`

- **What:** Late June into July is peak squash vine borer adult (clearwing moth) activity in PA. Adults lay eggs at the base of squash/zucchini/pumpkin stems; larvae bore in and cause sudden wilting.
- **Why it matters (for you):** This is the moment to act — inspect stem bases, consider row cover (remove at flowering for pollination), wrap/foil lower stems, or apply a labeled control. A vine that wilts overnight next week likely has a borer already inside.
- **Source:** Penn State Extension — vegetable pests (Tier 2) — https://extension.psu.edu/forage-and-food-crops/vegetables/pests-and-diseases

#### Tomato/potato late blight — monitor after Thursday's rain
`Heat: Medium` · `Momentum: NEW` · `Tier 1 — Confirmed` · `Category: Gardening`

- **What:** Penn State maintains a live PA late-blight tracker for the 2026 season. Warm, wet conditions like Thursday's storms favor infection on tomatoes and potatoes.

- **Source:** Penn State Extension — vegetable pests & diseases / PA blight reports (Tier 1) — https://extension.psu.edu/forage-and-food-crops/vegetables/pests-and-diseases

#### Spotted lanternfly nymphs active
`Heat: Low` · `Momentum: SUSTAINED` · `Tier 1 — Confirmed` · `Category: Gardening`

- **What:** SLF is in its nymph stage now (late-instar nymphs turn red with black and white spots in late June/July) and feeds on 70+ plant species, causing sap loss, wilting, leaf curl, and sooty mold. Mercer County sits within the broader PA infestation footprint.
- **Why it matters (for you):** Scout grape, tree-of-heaven, maples, and fruit trees; knock down/destroy nymphs and remove tree-of-heaven hosts where practical. Early nymph control is easier than fighting adults in fall.
- **Source:** PA Dept. of Agriculture / Penn State Extension — Spotted Lanternfly (Tier 1) — https://extension.psu.edu/spotted-lanternfly
