# Gaming Intelligence — Friday, April 10, 2026

## 1. Hades II Console Launch — 4 Days Away, Day-One Game Pass
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (Supergiant, Xbox Wire, TechRadar)**

Hades II console launch is now confirmed for Tuesday, April 14 on PS5, Xbox Series X|S and Cloud, with day-one availability across Game Pass Ultimate, Premium, and PC. Supergiant has confirmed the console versions will run at 120fps with new bonus content and quality-of-life improvements not present in the PC full-release build that shipped in September 2025. It will arrive with all post-launch patches included, making this the definitive version and the single most important gaming event of April. Hades II was the highest-rated game of 2025 on Metacritic and OpenCritic and peaked at ~587K concurrent on Steam at full launch. Current Steam CCU has fallen from that peak as expected pre-console — this pattern typically reverses into a second peak once Game Pass rollouts pull in players who waited. Xbox Play Anywhere support and a shared save layer mean a single Game Pass subscription covers both console and PC.

## 2. Game Pass April Wave 1 Expansion — Planet Coaster 2 + Tiny Bookshop Live
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: NEW | Source: Tier 1 (Xbox Wire, GameSpot)**

The Game Pass April wave continues rolling out. Planet Coaster 2 went live yesterday (April 9) on Cloud, Xbox Series X|S and PC via Game Pass Ultimate, Premium, and PC Game Pass, strengthening the catalog's simulation/management presence at a time when PS Plus lacks an equivalent offering. Tiny Bookshop joins today (April 10) across the same tiers. These are secondary to the Hades II flagship but matter for the subscription-value argument Microsoft is making this month: Game Pass is positioning April 2026 as its strongest showcase month of the year, with 17+ titles arriving through the month and five day-one additions highlighted. The Sony dynamic-pricing backlash continues to widen the perceived value gap.

## 3. Starfield PS5 — Settling Below Expectations After Launch Spike
**Heat: Medium | Momentum: DECLINING | Source: Tier 1 (SteamDB, Push Square, Metacritic), Tier 3 (community reaction)**

Three days past its PS5 launch, Starfield has not broken out of its ceiling. The PS5 version debuted at #16 on the PlayStation Store chart on launch day, and Steam concurrency — which spiked to ~19,914 on April 7 on the Free Lanes + Terran Armada drop — is already normalizing. Metacritic holds at 88 from critics but Steam user reviews stay at ~55% positive across 116K reviews. The Free Lanes update and Terran Armada expansion are credited as "the best version yet," but the core loop critique (loading screens, empty planets, outdated design) remains the dominant community sentiment. Bethesda continues to pitch a roadmap with "crazy" additions, but the April data suggests the game has found its steady-state audience and will not re-enter broader cultural conversation without a fundamentally different content drop.

## 4. Pragmata — Gone Gold, Previews Strongly Positive, 7 Days Out
**Heat: High | Momentum: RISING | Source: Tier 1 (Capcom), Tier 2 (RPG Site, CBR, MonsterVine)**

Pragmata, Capcom's long-delayed sci-fi action-puzzle hybrid, is confirmed gold and locked for April 17 on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC (Steam) and Switch 2 — all platforms simultaneously, including Switch 2 at launch. Recent extended previews from multiple outlets are meaningfully positive, pointing to a tight gearshift loop between Hugh's combat and Diana's hacking grid-puzzle mechanic. The 2M+ demo downloads Capcom reported pre-launch give it genuine pre-release awareness. After Monster Hunter Wilds and Resident Evil Requiem, Pragmata is positioned as the third major Capcom release of 2026 and the first brand-new Capcom IP launch in years — the breakout risk is real if the opening hours land. It is also Switch 2's highest-profile multiplatform AAA in April.
